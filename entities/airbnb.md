# Airbnb

Travel-marketplace company that appears in the podcast through [Laurent Charignon](laurent-charignon.md), who was a Staff engineer and, for a spell, an engineering manager there — a transition he pitched to his own overloaded manager rather than waiting to be asked ([Airbnb staff eng](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).

Two org traits stand out from his account. First, Airbnb's designer-led culture made low-level infrastructure work harder to sell internally, but produced outside-the-box wins — most memorably hiring actual firefighters and crisis-management professionals to run [incident management](../concepts/incident-management.md), a practice Laurent carried to every later job ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). Second, its performance machinery illustrates the "untold rules" of [calibrations](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md): locally-developed rules mutate over time, such as anyone changing roles less than ~2.5 months before cycle end getting an automatic "meets expectations" — a rule that cost Laurent a likely staff [promotion](../concepts/promotions.md) when he switched to management mid-cycle ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). He ultimately left partly because of the four-year equity cliff ([compensation](../concepts/compensation-and-equity.md)) and partly out of boredom once his team ran itself ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
