# Jia Chen

21-time hackathon winner, college dropout, co-founder of Sprint.dev, and content creator — the podcast's main example of a non-traditional early-career path ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).

## Career arc

Built her reputation winning 21 hackathons, often from a non-target school, using hackathons both as a skill-builder and a recruiting pipeline (recruiters at larger hackathons scout future interns and fast-track applications). College eventually dulled her creativity and passion for learning, and she dropped out — deliberately, not on a whim: she first set preconditions by building projects, testing the idea, and evaluating her technical skills and investor network. She then co-founded the startup Sprint.dev ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Winning hackathons comes down to team dynamics, judge interaction, and smart execution — not credentials or a complicated tech stack. Keep presentations short and let judges interact with the project; teams that talk about their tech stack often lose ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
- Pick teammates on skills and drive rather than credentials: past projects, GitHub activity, and ambition beat resumes — she has won with determined community-college students ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
- Splitting into separate frontend and backend teams and merging late usually fails; connect both early and iterate together ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
- From a non-target school, make yourself the outlier: take leadership roles, spearhead big projects, and build a personal brand ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
- Develop "agency" — the ability to take action and think on your feet — plus a "get it done and move on" time-efficiency mindset ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
- College provides structure if you don't know what to learn, but isn't necessary; don't drop out without preconditions in place ([non-linear careers](../concepts/non-linear-careers.md)) ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
- As a [founder](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md): move fast and continuously ship while avoiding burnout, and stay open to connections and learning that don't seem immediately useful ([episode](../sources/21x-hackathon-winner-turned-college.md)).
