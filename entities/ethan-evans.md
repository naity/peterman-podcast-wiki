# Ethan Evans

Retired [Amazon](amazon.md) VP (Prime Video, App Store, Twitch integration, Prime Gaming), now an executive career coach; the podcast's most-featured guest, with one episode on his own career ([Aug 2025](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)) and a second on [corporate politics](../concepts/corporate-politics.md) at large ([Mar 2026](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).

## Career arc

Let go twice around the dot-com bust for abrasiveness (the second time as a "layoff of one"); an interviewer's remark that truly valuable people get kept triggered him to deliberately rebuild his soft skills around questions and listening ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). At Amazon — which grew ~100x under him, an "escalator" he also climbed — his director promotion came from an outsized bet: he told his SVP "unless you order me not to do this, I'm going to do it" on the TiVo partnership, and the promo doc read "without Ethan, we wouldn't have had TiVo." His VP promotion took 2.5-3 years via his "magic loop": an explicit partnership where he did everything his manager needed in exchange for the manager staking credibility on the promotion. As Twitch integration liaison he gave up an 800-person org to influence CEO Emmett Shear with zero authority. He worked under both Bezos (a founder who could gamble the company — "how much is that? because I might want to spend it" — and felt emotionally supportive) and Jassy (a hired business leader whose probing style felt like waiting for him to fail) ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).

## Key views & advice

### Promotions and career growth
- Be "strategically annoying" rather than abrasive: push your opinions, but if people stop disagreeing to your face they organize behind your back ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- Express urgency without threats — "my career is very important to me, and I need to know if it's as important to Amazon" carries the same message as a quit threat without triggering the extortion defense; delivery, warmth, and timing determine how identical words land ([2025 episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md), [2026 episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). He admits leaders often promote the polite agitator over the patient team player because retention triage is real ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- Large companies run "forward looking slates" of [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md) over 6-24 month windows; quota systems (e.g. [Google](google.md) allowing two L8-to-L9 promos per half per org) create literal queues — million-dollar senior IC roles are not handed out "like popcorn" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).
- Reports bear the process risk: he once cost a star SDE1 a promotion by not knowing Amazon's twice-yearly promo cycle — verify your manager actually knows the process and deadlines ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- Quiet, dutiful high performers get unconsciously deferred for resources; "you are a very nice person... and that is not a compliment in this context" — state what you want ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).

### Corporate politics
- Empire building persists because headcount is the only promotion metric nobody can debate — some Amazon orgs required ~80-90 reports for director, "quite literally a several hundred thousand dollars a year bonus for accumulating 90 people" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).
- Reorgs are "hidden chess": a public business narrative coexists with private motives (retaining favorites with juicier scope, platforming someone for promotion, managing others out) wrapped in defensible storytelling ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).
- [Influence](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md) and politics are the same skill set distinguished only by motive ("Darth Vader had executive presence in spades"); treat cross-functional partners as people with competing pressures rather than APIs ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).
- Defending org scope: bundle unglamorous essential work into the story, respond quickly and thornily to raids, and "be the better storyteller" — attackers underestimate others' complexity (S3 dismissed as "just a big disk drive") because "respect between two engineering teams is inversely proportional to their distance" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).
- Never mutiny alone against a bad manager: a lone complaint is cheaper for the skip to dismiss than to believe; two or three corroborating reports flip the calculus. To escape a bad manager, first try a pure business case, then a blame-free framing ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).

### Performance management and firing
- Amazon's unregretted-attrition goal (~4-7%/year) returned after an experiment showed managers stop having hard conversations without it; PIPs are self-fulfilling — everyone he coached through one was fired anyway, so "spend that time on the PIP looking for your next job" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). Contrast [Bryan Cantrill](bryan-cantrill.md), who calls such [stack-ranking](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md) systems "organizational cancer" ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).
- A manager can fire any single person they choose: they strike first with the narrative (700 pull requests spun as upleveling or as nitpicking), and HR will not rescue you — "you're bringing a knife to a gunfight" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- VP reviews reward interim goals over the vision: he was rated well every year while Prime Gaming never approached its Tencent-scale vision; zombie products persist because shutting them down admits failure ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- Demanding high performers are easier to manage than nice ones: make explicit deals (deliver on my priorities and I'll deliver your promotion) and always follow through ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).

### Advice to his younger self
- Always prefer high-growth companies, and build reputation and relationships early so companies call you ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
