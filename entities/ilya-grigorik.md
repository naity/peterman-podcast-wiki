# Ilya Grigorik

Distinguished Engineer (L10) at [Shopify](shopify.md); previously a Director at [Google](google.md), web-performance authority, and founder of PostRank ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).

## Career arc

A Waterloo student who credits the co-op program's ~six stigma-free internships as the school's underappreciated superpower, he deferred (and never finished) grad school to found PostRank, a "better PageRank" ranking posts by social engagement ([startups and founding](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md)) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)). [Google](google.md) acquired PostRank during its race against Facebook on social — close to an acquihire, with the whole team (founder/CTO included) going through Google's full interview panel for leveling, and the product rebuilt from scratch inside Google Analytics at exabyte scale. Though told he was on track for director in Analytics, he took a lateral, likely down-leveled IC role in the "Make the Web Fast" group for self-direction and love of the domain — the right call in retrospect. His W3C/IETF browser performance-metrics work later created an adoption problem one person couldn't scale, so he became Director of Developer Relations to compress 3–5 years of ecosystem adoption into 1–2. He is now one of Shopify's most senior engineers as Distinguished Engineer (L10) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).

## Key views & advice

- "Don't be the best, be the only": build a portfolio of 80–90% competencies (design, sales, marketing, writing, management) whose intersection makes you uniquely capable and antifragile — "literally probability math" — and LLMs now make acquiring the first 80% faster than ever ([career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).
- Treats IC vs manager as "tours of duty" chosen per problem, not an identity ([management](../concepts/management.md)) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).
- Anyone who asks for a principal-engineer checklist isn't ready for the level: "the problems become ambiguous enough at that level where you kind of have to figure it out yourself" — you get parachuted into ambiguous terrain and must map problems, build alliances, and find where your skillset applies within about a week ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).
- His success test as a principal engineer is disappearing "Homer Simpson into the bushes": if the team keeps executing well without him, he did his job of upleveling them ([influence and leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md)) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).
- Distinguished Engineer is a VP-equivalent role defined by dynamic range — technically from bare metal to business requirements, and in execution style from fog-of-war iteration to slow platform API design with years-long consequences ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).
- Career regret: letting the pressure of past success filter what he publishes — early embarrassing writing and code were the feedback loop that drove his growth. "Don't let the pressure of your success stop you from trying" ([regrets and advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md)) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).
