# Ryan Peterman

Host of The Peterman Post, the podcast this wiki covers; a [Meta](meta.md) engineer and author of The Developing Dev newsletter. In the podcast's one behind-the-scenes episode he is interviewed by Brandon ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).

## Why the podcast exists

Ryan started the show as deliberate practice for speaking: publishing forces him to pore over every word in editing — "a very powerful commitment mechanism and a very powerful feedback loop," far stronger than Toastmasters — with a deliberately lax goal of one episode per month ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)). The format is modeled on internal Facebook career-story posts by very senior ICs — 50-minute reads he devoured early in his career — hence super-senior engineers telling their career stories ([teaching and communication](../concepts/teaching-and-communication.md)) ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).

## How he runs the show

- The [Philip Su](philip-su.md) episode was the first breakout — "the graphs were all going vertical" within a day or two — which Ryan read as product-market fit for hidden senior-IC career stories; the Ethan Evans episode confirmed the pattern, and Ryan calls Evans one of the best speakers he's seen in tech ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).
- Guest sourcing: he targets "hidden super senior engineers who no one knows at all" — famous only inside their orgs, unfindable via LinkedIn or Google — sourced through his big-tech network and by asking the audience which senior IC in their org impresses them ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).
- Trailer strategy: the aggressive Evans trailer worked so well he plans tasteful trailers on YouTube but minimal or none on Spotify/Apple, where recurring listeners live ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).
- Hardest host habit change: suppressing verbal backchannel ("uh-huh, right, yeah") and nodding vigorously instead, because acknowledgment noises ruin the audio ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).
- Balancing content with a full-time [Meta](meta.md) job is brutal: for about two years he stayed up until 4:00 AM every Thursday writing the newsletter, and Gergely Orosz's warning about "burning the candle at both ends" proved right ([career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)) ([episode](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)).
