# Austen McDonald

Former [Meta](meta.md) hiring committee lead for mobile, now an interview coach; the podcast's inside view of how senior+ engineering hiring actually gets decided ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).

## Career arc

Led hiring committees at Meta, reading thousands of candidate packets for senior+ engineers before leaving to coach candidates full-time. His episode is less a career story than a systems tour of the FAANG [hiring](../concepts/hiring-and-interviews.md) pipeline: sourcer → phone screen → onsite → committee (hire/no-hire + level) → director approval ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Committees open a senior packet at the behavioral interview first, because scope, influence, and insight determine leveling far more than coding; for staff+ candidates, technical rounds become pass/fail checkmarks while behavioral rounds differentiate ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Initial leveling is set by years of experience and previous title: FAANG titles map roughly one-to-one, non-FAANG titles get downleveled significantly for level inflation ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Fabricating seniority essentially never works — calibrated interviewers expose vague or AI-generated stories with follow-ups; he never saw a confirmed successful liar in thousands of interviews ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Every story should hit three dimensions — technical depth, organizational depth, business impact — and a crisp tell-me-about-yourself is the highest-leverage prep. Story selection priority: scope > relevance > recency > uniqueness across rounds ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Recruiters "are not your friend": the recruiter call is an evaluating call; avoid early [compensation](../concepts/compensation-and-equity.md) anchoring, use CA/NY salary-band laws, and hold multiple simultaneous offers as the main lever ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- You can negotiate level mid-process: get the loop run at the target level, anchor every interviewer in your opening, and answer the "question behind the question" with your highest-scope stories ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Referrals matter most on the bubble — a VP who worked with you stakes reputation and can advocate in the committee room (draft the referral content for them) — but no referral overrides unanimous no-hire consensus for engineers ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Meta's behavioral rubric has five signals (driving results, embracing ambiguity, resolving conflicts, growing continuously, communicating effectively) built by industrial-psychology "selection scientists"; get the rubric from your recruiter beforehand ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- AI-lab tailoring: [OpenAI](openai.md)'s "Feel the AGI" wants genuine optimism; [Anthropic](anthropic.md)'s "Hold Light and Shade" wants articulation of both positive and negative implications and evidence you mitigated risks ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Likability bias scales with level: rubrics suppress vibes for juniors, but senior roles are about influencing people, so confidence and "casual excellence" legitimately affect outcomes ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
