# Adam Ernst

Distinguished Engineer (IC9) at [Meta](../entities/meta.md) working on iOS infrastructure — a self-described "coding machine" whose frameworks (MemModels, ComponentKit) shaped how the whole company builds iOS apps ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).

## Career arc

Ernst ran a software company (Cosmic Soft) in middle school, selling REALbasic online-testing software to teachers worldwide via mailed $19.95 checks. He joined Facebook as an E5 weeks before the 2012 IPO, when every iOS engineer fit in one conference room, during the native rewrite away from HTML5 ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)). His E6 promo came from replacing [Apple](../entities/apple.md)'s Core Data — which "falls over completely" at Meta scale — with the immutable MemModels system; his IC7 promo project was ComponentKit (2014), which brought React's concepts to iOS before Swift, SwiftUI, or React Native existed, nudged by manager Lee Byron (GraphQL co-inventor). He then spent two years on ComponentScript, a cross-platform framework that was "a total and complete failure" despite technical excellence, earning a Meets Most rating before he killed it responsibly — migrating every feature off, deleting it from the repo, and publishing a public postmortem. He stayed on the IC track to IC9, never wanting [management](../concepts/management.md) ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).

## Key views & advice

- [Influence](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md)-without-authority playbook: talk in person, open by sympathizing with the other side, do deep research (his team disassembled Core Data's closed source to prove why it couldn't scale), and "do the work for them" so teams only have to say yes ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- Code review is massively undervalued: he reviewed 1,600+ diffs in a half (~14 per workday) as an organic, viral channel of influence; good comments explain *why* and stay flexible ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- From the ComponentScript failure: "just because it was technically excellent, didn't mean it was going to win." Target a specific engineer persona, land one committed anchor team before going wide, and know when refusing to compromise (he held out for GraphQL data consistency; a server-driven rival skipped it and won) costs you the war ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- Kill failing projects responsibly — "you might get more goodwill from killing it responsibly than dragging it out"; his coding-machine reflex of "just write more code" was exactly the wrong response to a failing project ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- Depth-plus-breadth method: when blocked by another team's system, dive "eight levels deep" into its guts and show up with the diagnosis instead of a ticket ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- On IC9 expectations he has "flipped the switch off": confirm with his manager that he's solving problems that matter, then work without meta-anxiety — the worry itself is counterproductive ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- His only career [advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md): do what you love — specifically, like solving problems — because enthusiasm can't be faked and output, curiosity, and deep digging are downstream of it ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
