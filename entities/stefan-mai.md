# Stefan Mai

Former Senior Manager (M2) at [Meta](meta.md) and Software Development Manager at [Amazon](amazon.md); co-founder of interview-prep company Hello Interview with [Evan King](evan-king.md) ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).

## Career arc

His first week at [Amazon](amazon.md) (2012, early self-service advertising) began with his manager committing the team to a 6–8 month nights-and-weekends death march; 50–60 hour weeks were common for ambitious big-tech engineers ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). His manager transition came prematurely and took ~18 months of failing — the core mistake was not knowing how to get reports to reveal their real goals, fixed via active listening instead of transactional "API-style" one-on-ones. He left Amazon, transitioned into AI/ML, and rose to Senior Manager (M2) at [Meta](meta.md), where he administered the performance machinery he now dissects publicly. The moment his director knew he'd succeed: walking in with an explicit written plan listing things he didn't yet know how to do. He later co-founded Hello Interview ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Storytelling is a bedrock [manager](../concepts/management.md) skill: narrate the team's trajectory and define winning — a team that feels it's winning avoids zero-sum [politics](../concepts/corporate-politics.md), which fester exactly when winning slows ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Manager-of-managers is a different job with real "mutiny" risk: "if you assume that leading other managers is going to be leading engineers... it'll just be mutiny." In flat orgs, leaf managers advance by displacing those above them via skip-level griping ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Senior leaders have fewer, more binary levers: you cannot do an underperforming manager's job for them; at the top, underperformance means being let go, not coached ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Managers are not layoff-proof: span-of-control reductions put them first on the chopping block ([layoffs](../concepts/layoffs.md)) ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- On [performance systems](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md): Amazon and Meta converge — public ratings plus a private VP/director process picking linchpins; Meta pays its top quintile dramatically more (some cleared 2x peers). The process costs ~$15,000 per engineer per cycle, but "the integrity of the performance review process is the whole ball game" ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Low-performer quotas are universal at scale (roughly 7–15%, up to 20% pushed to the bottom bucket). PIPs are "first and foremost a legal paper trail," but people genuinely escape them because the trail loses credibility if nobody survives; Amazon HR was savage box-checking while Meta HR exhausted every avenue ([big tech culture](../concepts/big-tech-culture.md)) ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- AI cheating has made remote [interviews](../concepts/hiring-and-interviews.md) "horrible"; he expects assessments to evolve (low-level design, AI-allowed assignments) rather than anti-cheat to win — and cheaters get destroyed by performance management once hired ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Job hopping helps ambitious juniors but is a red flag at senior levels (staff+ moving every 18–24 months gets second-guessed); deep tenure's credibility snowball can become a shackle ([career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)) ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Advice to his younger self: find [mentors](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md) instead of going alone, and write explicit plans with high agency ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
