# Sash Zats

Ex-[Meta](meta.md)/[Instagram](instagram.md) Staff Engineer (IC6), known as a prototyper on Threads and AI teams; left Meta for startups ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).

## Career arc

Sash switched teams 10 times in 9 years at [Meta](meta.md) — iOS on Newsfeed Delight, an experimental hardware team, [Instagram](instagram.md)'s blockchain/NFT team, Threads pre-launch, AI prototyping teams — choosing for "pure fun" and zero-to-one ambiguity rather than impact, and still grew from IC4 to Staff (IC6) as a byproduct ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)). On the hardware team he landed only ~2 diffs in 6 months; the team was explicitly reviewed on de-risking directions, showing how big tech protects risk-takers from performance-review downside. He was recruited to Threads pre-launch on his prototyping reputation and immediately built a working Instagram-to-Mastodon (ActivityPub) bridge and a stripped-down text-first Instagram iOS app over Christmas break; Threads went from first prototype to public launch in ~5–6 months and became Meta's poster child for lean product building. He left Meta for [startups](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md) because it's a two-way door: financially irrational but a real test of what your ideas are worth, and big tech will hire you back ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Prototypes are the highest-fidelity communication medium: "you can have 500 decks or you can have one prototype that works... and all of a sudden people jump on the project." His GPT-3-era RAG demo over Facebook's help FAQ drew ~50 cold reach-outs across the company — visibility documents can't buy ([influence and leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md)) ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).
- Comfort in ambiguous, poorly defined spaces is a differentiating asset in big tech, where many strong engineers need clear specs before they can execute ([career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)) ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).
- Products usually fail on misaligned business incentives rather than technology — the lesson of the Instagram blockchain team, which died with crypto winter ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).
- Goodhart's law makes any chosen metric degrade as people game it, and much of what matters isn't measurable — but large companies still need frameworks because nothing else scales ([calibrations and ratings](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md)) ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).
- Don't obsess over fixing weaknesses: "position yourself where you can showcase your strength and where your weaknesses are not getting in your way" ([regrets and advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md)) ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).
