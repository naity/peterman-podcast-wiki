# Mark Zuckerberg

Founder and CEO of [Meta](meta.md); not a podcast guest, but a recurring figure in the firsthand accounts of early-Facebook engineers who worked directly with him ([working-with-legends](../concepts/working-with-legends.md)).

The most vivid stories come from [Michael Novati](michael-novati.md), who joined in 2009 when Facebook had ~200 engineers: he built emoji-reactions-on-any-post *with* Zuckerberg at a 2009 hackathon — the code was too bad to ship, but Zuck's idea became the industry norm — and during the 2010 Google Plus lockdown made a company-wide bet that got Zuckerberg to personally merge a PR, in exchange for Novati giving up riding ripsticks in the office ([Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)). When executives fretted over Novati's provocative internal newsletter, Zuckerberg told him directly to keep posting ([same](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)). [Philip Su](philip-su.md), who worked with both Zuckerberg and John Carmack, says what distinguished Zuck was his "constant devotion to growth" ([Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)). The executive machinery around him is blunter: [Stefan Mai](stefan-mai.md) notes that at SVP level the levers are binary — "if Mark has an SVP who's just not making the cut, he's gonna let them go," not run a coaching program ([Stefan Mai](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
