# Boris Cherny

Creator of Claude Code at [Anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md); previously Principal Engineer (IC8) at [Meta](../entities/meta.md)/[Instagram](../entities/instagram.md), TypeScript book author, and serial startup founder ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).

## Career arc

An economics-major dropout and serial [startup](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md) founder, Cherny joined Meta deliberately under-leveled — which he calls lucky: space to build without box-checking [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md) pressure. Side quests drove his growth: Undux became Facebook's most popular state-management framework after he scraped internal support groups, tallied complaints by team, and gave 20–40 tech talks biking around campus; he also wrote a TypeScript book and ran SF's biggest TypeScript meetup. IC6 came from volunteering Facebook Groups as guinea pig for the Comet rewrite without asking permission; IC7 from the public-groups "one-line change" with gnarly data-model implications. He moved to rural Japan for his wife's dream job, joined [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md)'s Tokyo landing team, and turned zero-timezone-overlap into a superpower — no meetings, massively more coding — fueling the Python-to-Hack migration he seeded by befriending the strongest skeptics over food and beer. His IC8 was a non-event ("Will said 'I think we should put you up for IC8', I said cool"). At [Anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md) he created Claude Code ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Don't push for level+1 when switching companies: coming in under-leveled let him crush expectations and compound reputation ([career-growth](../concepts/career-growth.md), [episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- "Build for the model six months from now, not today" (manager Ben Mann's advice): Claude Code was mediocre until Sonnet/Opus 4 landed; now ~80–90% of Claude Code is written with Claude Code, and Anthropic's per-engineer productivity rose ~70% despite the company tripling ([ai-coding-tools](../concepts/ai-coding-tools.md), [episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- On AI slop: hold model code to exactly the same bar as human code — vibe coding is for prototypes, pairing-with-plan-mode is the default, and he still hand-writes the core query loop ([ai-era-engineering](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md), [episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- "Levels don't matter at all," and on [imposter syndrome](../concepts/imposter-syndrome.md): "if you don't [feel it], then you're not pushing yourself hard enough" ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- Automation philosophy: tally every repeated code-review comment and write a lint rule after a few repeats — he eventually automated most of his own reviews; a problem hit 2–3 times is the top of the side-project funnel ([developer-tools](../concepts/developer-tools.md), [episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- Delegation rule (from Andy Grove's High Output Management): "You always delegate the thing you *do* want to do and that you know well because then you can monitor the progress" — as with handing the Hack migration to Jake Bolam ([influence-and-leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md), [episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- Scoping technique: time-box scoping to ~30 minutes, give experts a straw man rather than an open question; for a hard two-team data-model merge he ran a three-hour architecture "design competition" that produced 80%-identical designs — instant consensus ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- The one book he recommends: Functional Programming in Scala — "thinking in types" changed how he codes more than anything else ([functional-programming](../concepts/functional-programming.md), [programming-languages](../concepts/programming-languages.md)); on competitors, he doesn't use their products and tells the team more competitors is a sign of success. [Advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md) to his younger self: "Just use common sense" — big companies systematically pull you away from it ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
