# David Singleton

CTO of [Stripe](stripe.md) for 7 years; before that ~12 years at [Google](google.md) rising to VP of Engineering; now founder of AI startup /dev/agents ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).

## Career arc

His engine at [Google](google.md) was agency: as a junior engineer he removed the google.com-to-google.com/m mobile redirect nobody wanted to touch, saving ~70 years of user time per year ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)). He stayed nearly 12 years because every 18–24 months he realized he was still learning, moving from IC into [management](../concepts/management.md) and up to VP — a jump he describes as leading functions beyond engineering (design, product, partnerships) and losing the confidence of being able to do every job in the org. He joined [Stripe](stripe.md) by asking Patrick Collison for advice on a startup idea ("anytime you want money, ask for advice; anytime you want advice, ask for money"), which Collison converted into recruiting. After seven years as Stripe CTO he left to found /dev/agents ([startups and founding](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md)) ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Management and IC are different jobs: if you try management, do it full time, ideally somewhere with a trial-and-return path; he first tries to talk people out of it — it only fits if you draw energy from getting more out of others ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- Even the most senior leaders should experience the team's work at the "one foot level": his quarterly "engineer-acation" meant ~3 days embedded on a team shipping a small real project plus a friction log, which fed his developer-productivity strategy as CTO ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- "Teams that are perfectly happy are rarely performing at their very best potential": he won Google's team-voted Great Manager Award and later concluded he didn't deserve it, aiming instead for "type two fun" and insisting low performers be managed ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- Leaders must "continually step onto the balcony" — off the dance floor to look at how the whole org is working ([influence and leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md)) ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- [Communication](../concepts/teaching-and-communication.md) improves only with deliberate reps (his manager booked him 1000-person keynotes); senior leaders are "always on stage"; circulate drafts of important writing to reviewers ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- Scaling as a leader means designing org systems like an operating system: Stripe's nested weekly ops reviews were modeled directly on [Amazon](amazon.md)'s (Charlie Bell walked him through it); a process is only legitimate while clearly net positive for those inside it ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- Far-timezone engineering sites need complete missions and autonomy; splitting one team across Mountain View and London is the worst setup ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- Culture must be deliberately invested in from day one: Stripe's operating principles are distilled from the most effective employees' behaviors and enforced through exemplification and systems like [hiring](../concepts/hiring-and-interviews.md) and blameless [incident review](../concepts/incident-management.md) ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
- Senior hires almost always begin as advice conversations with people happy elsewhere ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)).
