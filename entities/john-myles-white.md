# John Myles White

Former Director of Engineering at [Meta](meta.md) Superintelligence Labs (PyTorch) and a core contributor to the Julia programming language ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).

## Career arc

White joined Meta's Core Data Science as an IC4 just as the "emotional contagion" PNAS paper became the company's worst PR disaster of the year; later, when all senior engineers on the experimentation team left within six months, he jumped from "random IC5" to de facto TL of Deltoid 3, Meta's A/B testing framework — his lesson being that sudden-vacuum opportunities are underrated ([career-growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)) ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). He rose to Director of Engineering on PyTorch, taking ~20% less [compensation](../concepts/compensation-and-equity.md) to work somewhere he was proud of ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). Before Meta he was a Julia core contributor — a language built by MIT math people "to destroy MATLAB" ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Bullish on Meta as a stockholder, bearish on being an employee: the engineer labor market flipped to perceived oversupply (except frontier-lab AI researchers), so employers make fewer retention sacrifices and [layoff](../concepts/layoffs.md) fear dominates employee stress ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- [Promo](../concepts/promotions.md) culture pathology: in Meta AI Infra literally everyone's first goal was promotion; teams shipped projects everyone believed were bad ("I have to ship it because that's my promo bar") and built maximally coupled systems to hit scope bars, decoupling promotion from skills that compound ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- You can't unilaterally disarm ("it's totally irrational not to play the game") but you can choose teams: PyTorch selected for love of the craft and held such a high leveling bar its IC8s beat other teams' IC10s — which ironically made its promo cases more credible ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- On [language performance](../concepts/programming-languages.md): "any code that's slow is slow because it's doing stuff it doesn't need to do" — naive R translations of C loops run 1,000-10,000x slower because dynamic languages pay for what users MIGHT do; fast languages have invariants, slow languages have runtime checks ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- Internal big-tech infra is a repeatable startup playbook: Statsig is essentially Meta's Deltoid and Airflow literally is Meta's DataSwarm; he regrets not building a Deltoid startup years earlier ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- Statistical illiteracy plus goal culture is dangerous: he used [manager](../concepts/management.md) authority over ratings to stop a team from shipping a goal-hitting change on July 1 and deleting it July 2 ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- Biggest [regret](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md): never attending skip-level office hours held by Javier Olivan (now Meta COO) despite weekly prompting — directors+ are starved for ground truth; if a leader asks you to show up, go. Advice to his younger self: be more ambitious — successful people "are not actually doing that much better than you. They just tried and you didn't try" ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
