# Meta

Meta (formerly Facebook) is the podcast's center of gravity: host Ryan Peterman is a Meta engineer, the show's format was inspired by internal Facebook career-story posts by very senior ICs ([Behind the Scenes](../sources/behind-the-scenes-of-this-podcast.md)), and 24 episodes feature current or former Meta/Instagram engineers, managers, and executives. Together they form the most complete public picture of Meta's IC ladder (IC3–IC10), its PSC performance machinery, and its engineering culture from pre-IPO Facebook to the Superintelligence Labs era.

## What the episodes reveal

### Promo & calibration system

Meta's ladder runs from IC3 (new grad) through IC9 (Distinguished) and beyond, with a parallel M1/M2 management track (M2 ≈ IC7, per [Stefan Mai](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). The mechanics recur across episodes:

- Ratings measure delivered impact; [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md) lag and measure *sustained next-level behaviors*. Marius Schulz earned "Redefines Expectations" for each of his three promos and describes the "laddering" logic calibrators use — there is "an IC4, IC5, and IC6 way to solve almost any problem," and doing next-next-level work is what separates "redefines" from "greatly exceeds" ([Instagram IC7 episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)). Simon Kindström's two "Redefines" ratings came from persistence through failed experiments and converting revenue impact into discretionary equity ("secret bonuses") ([26-year-old IC6](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- Above IC6 you need a business-shaped hole: "an IC7-shaped hole that can only be plugged by an IC7-shaped puzzle piece" ([Marius Schulz](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)); at IC8+ consensus is built *before* the [calibration](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md) room — Adrien Friggeri socialized his IC8 case with VPs a cycle early and recruited a critical D2 for monthly check-ins so "you don't have a debate in the room" ([New Grad to IC8](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)). At IC9, promotion is qualitative: "you need the judgment of someone at a higher level to explain what makes that person's work special" ([Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)).
- The manager's-chair view: Meta and [Amazon](amazon.md) converge on similar systems — public ratings plus a private senior-leader process (discretionary equity) — but Meta pays its upper quintile dramatically more (some engineers cleared 2x their peers). Low-performer quotas of roughly 7–15% ("meets some/meets most") are real, PIPs are first a legal paper trail but escapable, and Meta HR (at the time) exhausted every avenue before termination ([Stefan Mai](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- The system's dark side, dated to the layoff era: John Myles White, who ran promos in AI Infra for years, says promo culture there meant literally everyone's first goal was promo — teams shipped projects everyone agreed were bad and built systems deliberately maximally coupled to hit scope bars; PyTorch's countermove was holding its promo bar so high that outside orgs trusted its cases (2026 episode, [MSL Eng Director](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). Ryan Olson is similarly candid that levels are "an incentive system... a carrot that's being dangled," with an unwritten ~1-promo-per-half pacing ([Instagram Stories episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)). Against this, Sash Zats reached IC6 while optimizing for fun over impact ([10 team switches](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)), and Boris Cherny argues coming in *under*-leveled was lucky — space to build without box-checking ([Boris Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- Demotion is asymmetric: Philip Su successfully requested IC9→IC7 to do more technical work ([Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)), but when Igor asked to drop E7→E6 in 2025, HR said Meta has no process for lowering a level within the same job category and refused — so he boomeranged to [Google](google.md) at L6 instead ([demotion story](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)).
- [Hiring](../concepts/hiring-and-interviews.md) mirrors calibration: hiring committees open the behavioral interview first to read scope; a Google staff engineer slots as Meta staff while non-FAANG "principal architects" get downleveled; the behavioral rubric (five signals, designed by industrial-psychology PhDs) can be obtained from your recruiter in advance ([Austen McDonald](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)). Igor adds the flip side: Meta doesn't know how to onboard senior external hires, because nearly all its senior people grew up internally ([demotion story](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)).

### Engineering culture

- Early Facebook was extremely engineer-driven: PMs and designers had to win engineers over, nearly all internal tools were built in-house, and "move fast and break things" meant breaking *norms*, not recklessness ([Michael Novati](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)). The six-week bootcamp had VP-level hires fixing bugs next to new grads — everyone in engineering stays technical ([Rong Yan](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)). Fiona Fung, arriving from [Microsoft](microsoft.md), was struck by one-week sprints (vs. four on Visual Studio) and the poster "nothing at Facebook is somebody else's problem" ([Anthropic eng leader](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)); David Fowler's "you can just do things" permissionless agency has a Meta twin in Bolin's Buck and Cherny's Undux, both unsanctioned projects that became company infrastructure ([Michael Bolin](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md), [Boris Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- Guests disagree about how intense it really is. Igor (2026) found the pressure performative: deliberately aggressive, arbitrary deadlines that slip while "everybody is fine with that — like elementary school, where the teacher is yelling but kids are still playing," versus Google applying pressure only in exceptional cases ([demotion story](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)). But 2016-era product sprints were real: Ryan Olson worked 16–18 hour days shipping Stories in ~3 months ([Instagram Stories](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)), and Fiona Fung's Marketplace war room ("we won't exit until we hit the number") dragged 2+ months ([Anthropic eng leader](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)). White dates the shift: the labor market flipped and Meta now makes fewer retention sacrifices — he's "bullish on Meta as a stockholder, bearish if you're an employee who's not" ([MSL Eng Director](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)); Novati left when the "all-in" cult-like mutual loyalty faded into a business relationship ([Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- Meta as tooling pioneer: it hit the scaling limits of every mobile developer tool before everyone else — hence Buck, Nuclide, the Eden VFS ([Michael Bolin](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md)); Hack, the typed PHP that became the company's most-used language, whose migration to ~100% strict typing spanned thousands of engineers ([Dwayne Reeves](../sources/tech-lead-for-metas-most-used-programming.md)); ComponentKit, React-for-iOS before Swift or React Native existed ([Adam Ernst](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)); and Bento, the company-wide notebook platform grown from one engineer's helper scripts ([Adrien Friggeri](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)). Internal playbooks leak out as billion-dollar startups — White claims Statsig is essentially Deltoid and Airflow literally is Meta's DataSwarm ([MSL Eng Director](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). The internal agentic platform DevMate went so viral (40,000+ engineers wanting dev servers) it convinced James Everingham to found an AI-agent governance startup ([Ex-Head of Eng at Instagram](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)) — and using Claude via DevMate is what pulled Fiona Fung to [Anthropic](anthropic.md) ([Anthropic eng leader](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- Performance management as a strength: David Ronca, after 12 years inside Netflix's famous culture, says the most valuable thing Meta taught him was *objective* performance management — calibrations where managers argue with data, and PSC packets that permanently record individual achievement, the thing Netflix's team-first culture lacked ([Retired Netflix director](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)). During COVID his Facebook video org ran at New Year's Eve peak every day while 3.5B users depended on the apps ([same](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)).

### Archetypes & career paths

- The "code machine" archetype is a Meta signature: Novati was #1 committer company-wide, but says what actually gated IC7 was accumulated judgment and taste, not raw output ([Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)); Evan King and Ryan Peterman both self-identify as code machines ([Evan King](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)); Adam Ernst calls the archetype's failure mode by name — "I just need to write more code" was exactly the wrong response to his failing ComponentScript project ([Adam Ernst](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)). Other recognized archetypes: specialist, fixer, project-starter, product-hybrid ([Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md), [Marius Schulz](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- Meta produces unusually young staff engineers — IC6 by 25 (Evan King), IC6 by 26 (Simon Kindström), IC7 by 27 (Novati) — via outsized impact rather than technical complexity ([Evan King](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md), [26-year-old IC6](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- What IC7+ engineers share, per the IC7+-only internal group: extreme conscientiousness, attention to detail, and sharpness, regardless of archetype ([Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)). At IC9, expectations are defined by "blast radius" — Su made IC9 by scaling Facebook London from ~a dozen engineers to 500+ ([Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)); Ernst's IC9 view is to switch off the meta-anxiety entirely and work on what he loves once aligned with the org ([Adam Ernst](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).
- Meta is also a launching pad: its senior alumni built Claude Code ([Boris Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)), lead OpenAI's Codex ([Michael Bolin](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md)), and founded Retro, Formation, HeyGen, Guild, and Sei Labs ([Instagram Stories](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md), [Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md), [Rong Yan](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md), [Ex-Head of Eng](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md), [Jayendra Jog](../sources/quitting-robinhood-and-raising-35m.md)).

### Stories about Zuck

- Michael Novati built emoji-reactions-on-any-post *with* Zuckerberg at a 2009 hackathon (the code was too bad to ship; the idea became the industry norm), and during the 2010 Google Plus lockdown made a company-wide bet that got Zuck to personally merge a PR — in exchange for Novati giving up riding ripsticks in the office. When execs fretted over Novati's provocative internal newsletter, Zuck told him directly to keep posting ([Zuck stories](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- Philip Su, who worked with both Zuckerberg and John Carmack, says what distinguished Zuck was "constant devotion to growth" ([Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)).
- The executive machinery around him: Stefan Mai notes that at SVP level the levers are binary — "if Mark has an SVP who's just not making the cut, he's gonna let them go," not run a coaching program ([Stefan Mai](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). John Myles White's biggest regret is never attending office hours that his skip-level Javier Olivan (now Meta COO) held weekly ([MSL Eng Director](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
