# Snapchat

Snapchat (Snap Inc.) appears in two episodes: as the hypergrowth employer where Rong Yan became Senior Director, and as the competitor whose Stories format [Instagram](instagram.md) famously copied and beat.

## What the episodes reveal

- **Hypergrowth from the inside.** Snapchat grew from ~100 employees (Rong Yan was roughly the 100th) to ~3,000 in two years; his org reached ~250 engineers. His scaling law: "every single time you double your team, it's almost like you're building a new company," requiring leaders to abandon old cultural assumptions. He deliberately chose the camera org over the comfortable data org — learning iOS from scratch in two months — to close product-side gaps toward his CTO-of-an-AI-company North Star ([Rong Yan](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)).
- **Evan Spiegel stories.** Pixel-level product-review feedback reminiscent of Steve Jobs; a promise never to fire the first 15 engineers; buying back the original "Blue House" in 2017 to host board meetings there ([Rong Yan](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)).
- **The Stories war, from the other side.** Instagram Stories — built in ~2–3 months by a deliberately tiny team — launched polished enough to beat Snapchat at everyday sharing, helped by Instagram's unfair advantage, the existing friend graph ([Ryan Olson](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).
