# Evan King

Ex-Meta Staff Engineer (IC6) by age 25 — Junior (IC3) to Staff in three years — later a startup founder ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).

## Career arc

Went from Cornell (where he founded the Hacking Club) to [Meta](meta.md) as a junior engineer (IC3), reaching Staff (IC6) in three years by age 25. He was promoted in his first half by pursuing projects outside his core scope; his manager handed him borderline engineering-management responsibilities to scale a growing team. His Senior-to-Staff case came from leading the creation of Meta's real-time integrity team after the Christchurch incident, combining visibility and impact. An after-hours crypto side project gained 10k+ users and eventually led him to leave big tech for entrepreneurship ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Identifies (with host Ryan Peterman) as a "code machine" — a high-output engineer rather than a natural prodigy; he started programming late in high school ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).
- Impact trumps technical complexity: his Staff [promotion](../concepts/promotions.md) came from a simple project with extraordinary results, not technical sophistication ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).
- Show agency: pursue projects outside your core scope rather than waiting for opportunities to be assigned ([career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)) ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).
- Argues [startups](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md) promote faster technical growth than big tech: big tech offers depth, startups provide breadth ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).
- Soft skills from founding Cornell's Hacking Club proved instrumental to his leadership growth ([influence and leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md)) ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).
- Regrets: over-prioritizing work early at the cost of friendships and self-reflection, and taking promotion shortcuts that sacrificed deep technical understanding he later needed ([regrets and advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md)) ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)).
