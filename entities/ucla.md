# UCLA

University with a personal thread through the podcast: host Ryan Peterman was a student there, and [Carey Nachenberg](carey-nachenberg.md) — GoogleX Chief Scientist and Symantec Fellow — has lectured CS there for 25 years alongside his industry career; Ryan was his student ([GoogleX chief scientist](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)).

Two episodes anchor the connection. Nachenberg's interview treats UCLA as his teaching laboratory, including an [AI-era](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md) policy experiment he regrets: letting students use LLMs on projects made project scores diverge sharply from exam understanding, so he's banning them for projects while allowing them for learning ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)). And the "Industry Secrets" episode is a recorded Q&A talk Ryan gave at UCLA with Ricky (Staff at [Google](google.md) by 28) for UPE (Upsilon Pi Epsilon), the club both belonged to as students — over 250 RSVPs for a rapid-fire distillation of what two Staff engineers wish they'd known before graduating: luck surface area, visibility, [Big Tech](../concepts/big-tech-culture.md) before [startups](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md), and why perfect code isn't enough ([UCLA talk](../sources/industry-secrets-we-wish-we-knew.md)).
