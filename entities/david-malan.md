# David Malan

[Harvard](harvard.md) professor and instructor of CS50, the university's famous intro CS course and one of the world's largest online courses ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).

## Career arc

Malan was a government concentrator who took CS50 as a sophomore in 1996 under Brian Kernighan, got -2 on his first hello-world, and switched fields because "it was the first time in like 19 years that homework was fun" ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)). He took the course over in 2007 after his PhD — initially a one-year fill-in — and grew its online presence organically from VHS filming (1999) through podcasting and chained free hosting accounts to today's global scale, with CS50 variants at Yale (10 years) and Oxford ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)). His biggest [regret](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md): not exploring enough and never working in industry — his sabbatical as professor-in-residence at GitHub was so fun he wishes he'd done it decades earlier ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).

## Key views & advice

- [Lecturing](../concepts/teaching-and-communication.md) technique: "memorable moments" — tearing a phone book for binary search, students sorting on stage — theatrical anchors that knowingly trade depth for motivation; he rejects the MOOC 3-5-minute-chunk model because bite-sized content drives engagement, not educational outcomes ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- C remains the right first real language: near-hardware, English-like, no big standard library — students build hash tables and tries from scratch by week 5, then week 6 collapses it all into one line of Python, teaching what abstractions cost and enable ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- To "full-stack devs don't need this" critics: you won't USE these things but you need to KNOW them — first principles distinguish an engineer from a coder whose output AI can already generate ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- CS50's [AI](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md) policy: off-the-shelf ChatGPT/Copilot banned for problem sets, but unlimited CS50.ai — a deliberately less-helpful rubber-duck tutor tuned to lead rather than answer. AI hasn't increased cheating detections but makes prosecution harder (no smoking-gun URL) ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- On studying CS now: "the whole point of so many of these courses has been about getting better at problem solving," not programming; AI eats the un-fun parts while humans keep system/UX/data design, and the enrollment decline is another pendulum swing like the dot-com bust ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- Education wastes enormous redundant effort, but consolidation fails on school pride — even during COVID he couldn't get Harvard to cross-list courses with [MIT](mit.md)/Stanford/Yale ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- Pointers are the perennially hardest concept; his prescription is metaphors and time — "take 24 weeks, take 52 — time is a perfectly reasonable knob to turn." Advice to his younger self: explore more, calm down ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
