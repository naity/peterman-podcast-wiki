# Brian Attwell

CTO of CloudKitchens; previously Senior Staff engineer at [Uber](uber.md) by age 25, with earlier stints at [Google](google.md) and an [Apple](apple.md) internship ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).

## Career arc

As an [Apple](apple.md) intern he accidentally killed his own sunlight-readability project by downplaying it in front of Steve Jobs, who rolled his eyes and walked away ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)). At [Uber](uber.md) he reached Senior Staff by 25 through 90–100 hour weeks: he and a peer prototyped the options their team debated each day after hours, becoming de facto tech leads within months and top-3 committers across Uber mobile. The cost was real — he once collapsed on a street walking home, unable to move for 15 minutes. At CloudKitchens he procrastinated on [management](../concepts/management.md) so badly a report asked after three months whether Brian was actually his boss; once committed, his org doubled every six months, driven by assuming every inherited org has an order-of-magnitude improvement and by [truth-seeking](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md) with the team. As CTO he pursues the company's thesis that in a few decades no one should cook their own food (robots, logistics, real estate; Otter processes 18% of the world's orders) ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Career growth vs hours worked is superlinear, because strategic work only goes to the already-proven: "if you want to be senior staff at one of the world's best tech companies by the time you're 25, this is the only way to do it" ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- Luck dominates infrequent decisions (choosing a company every few years) but not high-frequency daily actions; picking a meritocratic culture is the biggest controllable-yet-lucky variable ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- Argues IQ correlating with job performance is among the best-researched results in applied psychology; LeetCode is a gameable IQ proxy, good interviews force on-the-spot reasoning (e.g. digest a 5-page postmortem in 5 minutes, then debate restructuring the team), and he is seriously considering literal IQ screens ([hiring and interviews](../concepts/hiring-and-interviews.md)) ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- Prioritization formula: expected value × probability-of-success-with-vs-without-you ÷ your time cost — which often means skipping the well-staffed top priority to be the hero on priority #3 ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- Led CloudKitchens' leveling-ladder rebuild after Travis Kalanick rejected an Uber-style draft as "lazy... over-indexing on scope": ~1,000 real performance examples PCA-collapsed into 12 skill gradients rewarding truth-seeking over politics-rewarding competencies like influencing other teams ([promotions](../concepts/promotions.md), [calibrations and ratings](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md)) ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- On big-tech honesty: [Amazon](amazon.md)-style companies underpay top performers "because they can get away with it"; at [Google](google.md) managers described an unwritten policy of retaining idle smart people so competitors couldn't hire them. "If you have entropy in a system, standards will naturally fall over time until the company dies" ([big tech culture](../concepts/big-tech-culture.md)) ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- He doesn't regret unavoidable failures, only moments he knew the truth mid-project and stayed silent; advice to his younger self: be more courageous, less embarrassed, and possibly go to therapy ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
