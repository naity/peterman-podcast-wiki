# Fiona Fung

Engineering leader supporting the Claude Code team at [Anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md), after 11.5 years at [Microsoft](../entities/microsoft.md) (Visual Studio, TypeScript) and a Senior Director run at [Meta](../entities/meta.md) spanning Facebook Marketplace and AR/VR (Quest 2, Ray-Ban Stories, Horizon OS) ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).

## Career arc

Fung spent 11.5 years at [Microsoft](../entities/microsoft.md), leaving only after shipping TypeScript 1.0, recruited by a former Visual Studio colleague to build what became Facebook Marketplace — drawn by the mission and by escaping the "safe cocoon" of the Microsoft stack. At [Meta](../entities/meta.md) she rose to Senior Director across Marketplace and AR/VR operating systems. She wasn't job hunting when using Claude (Sonnet) through Meta's internal DevMate tool convinced her AI was "already here"; [Anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md)'s safety-first mission-orientation — visible from day-one onboarding, not just a tagline — pulled her from a VR role she loved to lead the team behind Claude Code ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).

## Key views & advice

- On impact vs team health: war rooms are valuable for focus but tax morale — her Marketplace growth war room ("we won't exit until we hit the number," 75 experiments at once) dragged 2+ months, and her [regret](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md) is not being realistic and upfront with the team about duration ([management](../concepts/management.md), [episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- First-time manager-of-managers: treat it as an explicit partnership ("what are you strong at, what am I good at") and build a "trust but verify" muscle — the "this is fine" dog meme is her nightmare ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- [Mentoring](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md) vs coaching are different modes: mentoring is listening plus advice; coaching is being a mirror so the person finds answers themselves. The best relationships have mentee-set goals and an explicit "what does success look like in 3/6 months" ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- 1:1 advice for ICs: move status to async and spend the live time on real conversations — learning, digging deeper, [career](../concepts/career-growth.md) ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- Dogfooding is her signature leadership practice — Visual Studio to build Visual Studio, selling household items to seed Marketplace's test launch, reproducing VR floor-height bugs at home, and now "using Claude Code to build Claude Code." She frames it as a manager's maker time and a trust-builder with engineers ([developer-tools](../concepts/developer-tools.md), [ai-coding-tools](../concepts/ai-coding-tools.md), [episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- The feedback that changed her career was feedback on *receiving* feedback: don't "debug" the giver in the moment — stay in read-only mode, listen, and sit with it until the next day, because making someone justify constructive feedback discourages them from ever giving it again ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- Kindness as an engineering value: "in a world where you can be anything, be kind" — crystallized during COVID when a report cheerfully freed her to take a rare FaceTime slot with her grandmother ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- Claude Code's iteration loop feels faster than even early Marketplace; preserving that agility as the team grows is her top concern ([ai-era-engineering](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md), [episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
