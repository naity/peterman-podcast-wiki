# Bryan Cantrill

Co-founder/CTO of Oxide Computer; formerly Distinguished Engineer at Sun Microsystems (DTrace) and CTO of Joyent, where he competed directly with [AWS](aws.md) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).

## Career arc

Rose to Distinguished Engineer at Sun by never goaling on rank — promotions were byproducts of doing the most important work, and his career highlights were hard bugs and "impossible" projects. Sun's DE promotion was a political "country club" vote by existing DEs; he escaped the politics only because Sun's CTO personally presented his case. He lived through the dot-com bust in which Sun lost 98% of its value and ran ~35 rounds of [layoffs](../concepts/layoffs.md) over eight years — engineers scripted the employee directory into a daily "obits" report. He quit Oracle about 45 days after its acquisition of Sun closed because he was ashamed to work there ("For Sun, I was embarrassed from time to time, but never ashamed. And with Oracle, I was ashamed."). At Joyent he competed against AWS, then co-founded Oxide, whose MVP — a full rack — took 3+ years; Oxide deliberately neither competes nor partners with [Nvidia](nvidia.md), focusing on general-purpose compute ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).

## Key views & advice

- On [calibrations and ratings](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md): stack ranking is "organizational cancer" — firing a bottom percentage incentivizes managers to keep dead weight as sacrificial fodder (he saw this at Sun) and "teaches you that your team are adversaries" when teams do the extraordinary things. Oxide has no ranks at all: "ranks are corrosive" ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)). Contrast [Ethan Evans](ethan-evans.md), who describes Amazon's attrition-goal rationale from the inside ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- On [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md): "I would much rather be under promoted than over promoted... I am not interested in doing work performatively." To engineers goaling on titles or financial independence: find meaning instead — he can introduce you to miserable Distinguished Engineers and miserable wealthy people, and even meaningful shipped goals produce "postpartum in engineering" ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).
- Bezos is "the apex predator of capitalism": [Amazon](amazon.md) hid AWS's great margins by refusing to break out revenue and relentlessly cutting prices from 2010-2015, convincing the world the cloud was a bad business — while Joyent, competing directly, knew the margins were excellent ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).
- On [founding](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md): when a VC asks "how can I help?" have a concrete answer — he asked for an intro to a failed similar startup's founder and learned it died from a tiny real market plus premature go-to-market scaling ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).
- Boom/bust advice for the [AI era](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md): cycles are endemic to software, so be your own judge — people said operating systems were done in 1996 and that you couldn't start a computer company in 2019; don't pick a space just because it's hot ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).
