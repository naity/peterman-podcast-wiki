# Simon Kindström

Meta Staff Engineer (IC6) at age 26, having gone from New Grad (IC3) to Staff in three years with two top "Redefines Expectations" ratings and discretionary equity ("secret bonuses") along the way ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).

## Career arc

Joined [Meta](meta.md) as a new grad (IC3) and reached Staff (IC6) in three years. A manager recruited him by explaining the business impact of the work and how it would lead to career growth; the work then drove multiple significant revenue wins for Meta, which converted directly into top [ratings](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md) and accelerated [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md), plus discretionary equity grants. He briefly tried management as a deliberate experiment, found it pulled him away from the technical work he loved amid organizational thrash, and transparently switched back to the IC track ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).

## Key views & advice

- His redefines-expectations work came from persistence through experimental failure: when experiments produced unexpected bad results, he investigated root causes, modified, and reran until he understood the failure or improved the results ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- [Writing well](../concepts/teaching-and-communication.md) was a major career lever: write about your accomplishments and put the impactful, interesting parts at the top — engineers commonly bury the lede in technical details their audience finds irrelevant ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- A closely-coaching manager with deep organizational knowledge and personal experience of rapid advancement was central to his rise ([mentorship and sponsorship](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md)) ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- The critical Staff-level skill was scaling himself. His delegation failure mode was abdicating ownership of others' outcomes and scrambling at review time; the fix: "delegate, don't abdicate" — keep accountability while empowering others to execute ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- Treats [management](../concepts/management.md) vs IC as an experiment you can run and reverse transparently, not a one-way door ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
