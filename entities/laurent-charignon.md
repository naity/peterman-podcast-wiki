# Laurent Charignon

Developer-productivity specialist who was a Staff engineer at [Stripe](../entities/stripe.md), [Airbnb](../entities/airbnb.md), and [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md) ([Meta](../entities/meta.md)), with earlier time at [Apple](../entities/apple.md) and a spell as an Airbnb engineering manager — the podcast's most concrete source on how [calibrations](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md) actually work ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).

## Career arc

After [Apple](../entities/apple.md) and Instagram/[Meta](../entities/meta.md), Charignon went to [Airbnb](../entities/airbnb.md), where he pitched his own transition into [management](../concepts/management.md) — going to his overloaded manager with a value proposition rather than waiting to be asked — then returned to IC once his team ran itself, and left partly over the four-year equity cliff ([compensation](../concepts/compensation-and-equity.md)). At [Stripe](../entities/stripe.md) he joined as "uber TL" over a developer-productivity org of 35, later 140, engineers, building credibility by embedding in each team for weeks, refusing "it worked at Facebook" as justification, and leading almost entirely by asking questions — he claims he never outright rejected a design in his tenure ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Untold rules of [calibrations](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md): local, mutating bureaucratic rules exist — e.g. anyone changing roles less than ~2.5 months before cycle end gets an automatic "meets expectations," a rule that cost him a likely staff [promotion](../concepts/promotions.md). Dispute them one-on-one with evidence, never in the calibration room where reputations are at stake, and first learn the story behind the rule ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- His "surprise factor" metric as a manager: before a review, he and the report each wrote the expected rating on paper and revealed simultaneously — and positive surprises are failures too, since an undervalued report should have been coached to take more risk earlier ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- A hard truth: "As a manager, you represent the interest of the company. You have to put the company first before your report" ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Why engineers get stuck at senior: the senior-to-staff jump is a bigger mindset shift than staff-to-senior-staff — staff means independently owning the full cycle (identify, pitch, solve, document) rather than solving problems others chose. To find staff-worthy problems, "channel your inner frustration" — Stripe institutionalized this as friction logs ([career-growth](../concepts/career-growth.md), [episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- When leadership wants to rank engineers by lines of code or PR counts, redirect: instrument the journey of a code change (think → write → CI → review → merge) to surface org-level friction instead of unfairly ranking individuals ([developer-tools](../concepts/developer-tools.md), [episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Situational-leadership [coaching](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md): match style (directing, selling, supporting, delegating) to the learner's mastery and motivation — directive coaching was suffocating a self-taught bootcamp mentee who became the strongest of his cohort once Laurent switched to supporting ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- "It's much better to say I don't know than to say something wrong" — confidently wrong claims destroy coworker respect; boredom is a signal something is wrong ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Airbnb's designer-led culture made infra harder to sell but produced wins like hiring actual firefighters to run [incident management](../concepts/incident-management.md) — a practice he carried to every later job. Most career-impacting book: Radical Candor ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
