# IBM

IBM appears in the podcast as a historical institution: the birthplace of cryptographic research and the mainframe-era CISC establishment, a standards-committee power, and a research career launchpad — usually described from decades past rather than the present.

## What the episodes reveal

- **Cryptography's corporate cradle.** Martin Hellman got interested in cryptography in 1968 while at IBM Research (Yorktown Heights) just after his PhD, where Horst Feistel — "the father of IBM's research in cryptography" — worked; he spent a year there before teaching at [MIT](mit.md) and then Stanford ([Martin Hellman](../sources/turing-award-winner-nsa-public-key.md)).
- **The pre-relational and CISC establishment.** Mike Stonebraker's Ingres story begins one year after Ted Codd's relational paper; IBM's own hierarchical (tree-structured) data systems were, even by IBM's admission, not general enough, which the relational model displaced ([Stonebraker](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)). David Patterson describes 1960s–70s IBM mainframes as microprogrammed computers — interpreters in hardware — the design orthodoxy that smaller companies imitated and that RISC overturned ([Patterson](../sources/turing-award-winner-tpu-vs-gpu-vs.md)).
- **Standards muscle.** C++ standardization was forced on Bjarne Stroustrup when IBM and HP representatives spent an hour twisting his arm in 1989 — their organizations couldn't depend on a corporate-owned language; he later ran "shuttle diplomacy" between IBM and [Intel](intel.md) on committee disputes ([Stroustrup](../sources/creator-of-c-bell-labs-negative-overhead.md)).
- **As an employer.** Rong Yan began as an IBM Research scientist before leaving research for engineering at [Meta](meta.md) — a deliberate move toward his CTO-of-an-AI-company North Star ([Rong Yan](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)). David Ronca counts his early years at an IBM division (sold to Siemens) among the "wasted" first six years at large failing companies ([Retired Netflix director](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)). Igor's mobility comparison: switching projects was fluid at [Google](google.md) and Meta, near-impossible at Intel and — reputedly — IBM ([demotion story](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)).
