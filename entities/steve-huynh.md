# Steve Huynh

Ex-Amazon Principal Engineer (~19 years at the company) and creator of the A Life Engineered YouTube channel on engineering careers ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).

## Career arc

Spent roughly 19 years at [Amazon](amazon.md), rising to Principal Engineer. His promotion project was overhauling Prime Video's outdated catalog infrastructure — a high-leverage system relied on by hundreds of dependent engineers. After Amazon he became a full-time career-content creator ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).

## Key views & advice

- [Interviews](../concepts/hiring-and-interviews.md): coding and system design tests "just get you in the door"; behavioral interviews determine leveling and cultural fit, so articulate your experience to match the target level ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
- Market dynamics: junior roles face limited demand while Senior+ roles see extraordinary demand, and competition concentrates in pre-screening rather than the interviews themselves ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
- Amazon has an explicit up-and-out policy for SDE1s — promotion within a timeframe or termination — which relaxes at higher levels ([big tech culture](../concepts/big-tech-culture.md)) ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
- On [layoffs](../concepts/layoffs.md): in his observation Amazon layoffs typically hit people lacking cultural fit rather than poor performers. To avoid them, communicate directly with your manager about expectations — early transparent feedback prevents surprises ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
- The Principal Engineer [promotion](../concepts/promotions.md) requires nearly two levels' worth of contribution simultaneously, with conflicting feedback along the way — start preparing early ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
- Job-hop frequently early in your career for rapid advancement, but favor stability once you reach Senior+, where growth requires sustained tenure ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
- Regrets: not switching teams earlier, and focusing too much on people-pleasing and external expectations ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).
