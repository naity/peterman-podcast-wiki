# Asian Dad Energy (anonymous chief architect)

Anonymous guest known by his YouTube pseudonym "Asian Dad Energy": a big tech chief architect laid off after ~25 years in the industry, previously a group technical director in digital consulting, now a YouTuber whose layoff vlog went viral ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).

## Career arc

He spent ~18 years climbing the digital consulting ladder — junior SWE → senior SWE → tech lead → application architect → enterprise architect → senior enterprise architect → group technical director — with his best growth period (senior SWE through senior enterprise architect) driven by strong [mentors](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md); director-level C-suite selling burned him out. He then jumped to a chief architect role at a big tech company, nearly tripling his [compensation](../concepts/compensation-and-equity.md) (base, bonuses, RSUs, ~15% ESPP discount), and worked there ~7 years until the [layoff](../concepts/layoffs.md). Learning through his network he was on the list, he did a "switcheroo" to put himself at the top in place of more junior engineers, buying them a few months. Big tech applications went nowhere in the cold market; he is now exploring semi-early retirement, YouTube, and small coding projects ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).

## Key views & advice

- On the layoff wave: companies under pressure to show returns on heavy [AI](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md) investment cut headcount to keep revenue flat while dropping costs — plus "PR points for saying how innovative they are in terms of the AI revolution" that pump the stock. He expects more layoffs ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- Consulting quality holds up despite non-technical clients because of engineering integrity, pride in craft, and firm reputation — "if it is a steaming pile of turds, it's your obligation to tell your client" their $20M investment is bad ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- Consulting splits into fly-by-night "get in, build, get out" projects (often sold just to keep the pipeline full) and multi-year retainers where institutional knowledge enables real quality; a key consultant skill is reading the client's [political topology](../concepts/corporate-politics.md) ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- Hostility toward consultants mostly stems from *management* consulting, whose headcount-cut recommendations make employees associate consultants with layoffs ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- Always take free money (ESPP discount, 401k match); with spare cash he'd buy index funds ([compensation-and-equity](../concepts/compensation-and-equity.md), [episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- On aging in tech: after 40, learning a new framework took weeks instead of days (his Olympic-gymnast analogy); he now uses AI tools to polish his code ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- [Speaking advice](../concepts/teaching-and-communication.md) for engineers: relax, don't fuse your identity with your subject, explain like the audience is five ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
- [Advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md) to his younger self: work hard, never stop learning, stay flexible — "if you can stay flexible, you'll survive" ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
