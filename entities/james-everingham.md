# James Everingham

Ex-Head of Engineering at [Instagram](instagram.md), Netscape veteran, VP Eng at [Meta](meta.md) (Novi crypto, dev infra), now co-founder of Guild ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).

## Career arc

Kicked out of Penn State at 18 with a 0.0 GPA while the university simultaneously hired him full-time — its first staff hire without a degree in 50+ years — because they already used his shareware; as a teenage phone phreaker the FBI traced him (he'd put his name on his tone-emulating software) and asked him to stop. Borland colleagues took him to Netscape pre-IPO, where he lived through losing the browser war to [Microsoft](microsoft.md) and helped open-source Mozilla — stripping SSL (legally a munition) and hand-censoring profane variable names with highlighters. He joined Instagram as head of engineering, later ran Meta's Libra/Novi crypto effort (~3,500 people, blocked by regulators) and Meta dev infra (~1,000 engineers), where his internal agentic platform DevMate went viral among 40,000+ engineers and inspired Guild, a governance control plane for enterprise AI agents. Three Netscape-era colleagues are still with him at Guild five or six companies later ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).

## Key views & advice

- "It's not a technology war, it's a distribution war": superior technology loses to distribution, as when Microsoft bundled IE free and killed Netscape's $35 browser revenue; when joining a company, understand which war it's fighting and treat job choice like a portfolio decision ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- Shrinking teams improves velocity: Instagram Stories shipped in ~3 months from a small, talent-dense, founder-led group with clear ownership and no cross-org dependencies, guided by Mike Krieger's "do the simple thing first" ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- CTO vs VP Eng: he and Krieger published their job descriptions — CTO owns technology/roadmap/external brand, VP Eng owns people/hiring/execution; companies asking for "a new CTO" almost always describe VP-of-Eng problems ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- On [mentorship](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md): don't ask for a mentor, offer help; relationships compound and "friends come and go, but enemies collect" ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- [Management](../concepts/management.md) philosophy: micromanage only briefly to fix broken teams; technology is a creative job — hire people smarter than you, focus them on outcomes, clear blockers, get out of the way ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- On [AI-era](../concepts/ai-era-engineering.md) startups: many are "building for the future past," filling gaps LLMs will soon close — "you build a product on a bus stop, not where the end of the line is" ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- Novi regret: after years re-architecting for regulators who still blocked it, he thinks they should have shipped and asked forgiveness ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- [Regrets](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md): time is the scarce commodity — ensure you're having good days; go where the smart people are (he spent 7 years in Pennsylvania, "like an actor never trying Hollywood"); max the 401k from day one; speak up more ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
