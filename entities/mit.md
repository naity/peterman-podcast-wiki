# MIT

MIT recurs in seven episodes as both a faculty home for the show's Turing-laureate and theory guests (Liskov, Stonebraker, Ryan Williams) and as the alma mater whose real value alumni debate.

## What the episodes reveal

- **What the degree is actually worth.** Dwayne Reeves, who went straight from MIT to pre-IPO Facebook, is blunt: MIT's value was *opportunity access*, not the education — equally capable colleagues without degrees only landed at Facebook by chance; CTO Bret Taylor took the MIT new-grad cohort to lunch ([Hack tech lead](../sources/tech-lead-for-metas-most-used-programming.md)). James Cowling values his MIT PhD differently: not more college but the formative experience of facing a problem no one on earth can answer — training to sit with uncertainty; most aspiring engineers shouldn't do one ([Dropbox's most senior eng](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)).
- **Research lineage.** Barbara Liskov built CLU, the Liskov substitution principle, and Viewstamped Replication at MIT, and stayed for the research freedom — "the gift is you can do whatever you want, the curse is you have to figure out what it is that you're doing" ([Liskov](../sources/turing-award-winner-data-abstraction.md)). Cowling's PhD in her field produced Granola (cited by Google's Spanner paper) and Viewstamped Replication Revisited ([Dropbox's most senior eng](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)). Mike Stonebraker moved to MIT after Berkeley and launched DBOS from there ([Stonebraker](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)); Ryan Williams proved his time-vs-space breakthrough as an MIT professor and preaches "you don't need permission to work on very tough problems" ([MIT complexity theorist](../sources/mit-complexity-theorist-on-leetcode.md)). Martin Hellman taught at MIT for two years before joining the Stanford faculty ([Hellman](../sources/turing-award-winner-nsa-public-key.md)).
- **Institutional self-preservation.** David Malan notes that even during COVID he couldn't get Harvard to let students take a Stanford/MIT/Yale course online — his exhibit for education's redundant-parallelism problem ([CS50 episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)). John Myles White credits MIT math people with designing Julia "to destroy MATLAB" ([MSL eng director](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
