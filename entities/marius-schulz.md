# Marius Schulz

Senior Staff Engineer (IC7) at [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md)/[Meta](../entities/meta.md), first engineer on Threads Web — a front-end specialist with a "burning passion for the web" who earned a "redefines expectations" rating for each of his three [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md) ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).

## Career arc

Schulz's years of no-matter-what weekly blogging got a recruiter to notice him; he joined [Meta](../entities/meta.md) at IC4. His IC5 promo came from leading a clearly-classified IC6 project (10+ engineers plus PMs/designers) as an IC4 — an opportunity opened by a senior engineer's paternity leave. He was blocked twice along the way: once for a behaviors miss (too pushy in cross-functional collaboration) despite a greatly-exceeds half, once by Meta's early-COVID promo freeze. IC6 came from a hard-deadline web project where he flexed between product-hybrid and coding-machine modes. When web stopped being a priority in his org he deliberately switched to Jake Bolam's IG Web team; then, recruited via a hush-hush manager intro, he was the sole engineer on Threads Web for its first 6–8 weeks, hiring and TL'ing the team through launch of threads.net — nearly 2,000 diffs in the launch year — making his IC7 self-review "the easiest I ever submitted" because attribution was unambiguous ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).

## Key views & advice

- [Ratings](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md) measure delivered impact; promotions (which lag) measure *sustained next-level behaviors*. When blocked: "you can't be defeatist" — keep doing next-level work, because the growth continues on paper even when the title doesn't ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- Doing next-*next*-level work separates "redefines" from "greatly exceeds"; calibration uses "laddering" to articulate why ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- "There's an IC4, IC5, and IC6 way to go about almost any problem": IC4 adds one React error boundary; IC5 audits and hardens the whole site; IC6 writes the direction-setting note plus lint rules so the whole company applies it without him ([influence-and-leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md), [episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- Luck matters, but "you can increase your luck surface area" — loudly position yourself as the go-to person for a domain, and place yourself where your specialty aligns with business priorities ([career-growth](../concepts/career-growth.md), [episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- IC7+ promos need a business-shaped hole: without "an IC7 shaped hole that can only be plugged by an IC7 shaped puzzle piece," delivery alone won't get you promoted ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- Ownership as DRI is recursive — delegating meaty pieces doesn't absolve the overall DRI; his [mentorship](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md) model is the driving instructor with a second steering wheel who gradually backs off ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- Quality hierarchy under deadline pressure: cut scope before cutting quality. Diff counts are only "directionally interesting" and gameable — not every IC7 diff needs IC7 complexity ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- He rejects the [management](../concepts/management.md) track and isn't pushing for IC8: he loves the technical work ("the demigod feeling... I can see the matrix"). Every level scared him before he reached it; [advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md) to his younger self: "Chill a little bit. It's gonna be fine" ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
