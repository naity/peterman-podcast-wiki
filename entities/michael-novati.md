# Michael Novati

Ex-Meta Senior Staff Engineer (IC7) by age 27 and the #1 code committer at Meta; the podcast's archetypal "code machine." Now co-founder of Formation ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).

## Career arc

Joined Facebook as an intern in May 2009 (~200 engineers), skipping a planned HCI PhD at the University of Washington. A code machine from day one, he merged two competing internal task tools in his first week and announced it to the whole company. His raw code output stayed roughly constant his entire career; what got him to IC7 by 27 at [Meta](meta.md) was accumulated judgment and taste — knowing which parts of the codebase are sensitive, how changes affect other teams' in-flight migrations, and when speed is safe. Signature IC7-scale wins were relentless solo migrations no team would have finished: manually refactoring away ~5–6,000 "Preparable" framework classes and eliminating every legacy direct-hit PHP endpoint. He left after ~10 years when mutual all-in loyalty faded into a business relationship and vesting logistics meant an ~80% take-home pay drop, then co-founded Formation ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).

## Key views & advice

- Trust is the code machine's currency: after burning credibility with a rushed bug, he publicly told the push team "if this breaks the site, I will resign" — seriously — to force his own triple-checking and rebuild trust ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- As IC7 he spent ~30% of focus on his team and ~70% on codebase-wide initiatives, and aggressively declined meetings and standups to protect coding time ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- First-hand Zuckerberg stories ([working with legends](../concepts/working-with-legends.md)): built emoji-reactions-on-any-post with Zuckerberg at a 2009 hackathon (unshippable code, but the idea became the industry norm), and during the 2010 Google Plus lockdown a company-wide bet got Zuck to personally merge a PR ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- On [LLMs](../concepts/ai-coding-tools.md): the Vim-to-VSCode leap repeated — he produces ~5x more code in Formation's ~500k-line codebase by prompting for code he already sees in his head. Near-term AI amplifies experienced engineers with strong taste; long-term, AI managing its own services behind API contracts may make human-readable code irrelevant ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- Common traits in Meta's IC7+-only group regardless of archetype: extreme conscientiousness, attention to detail, and sharpness ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- Self-admitted weakness: prioritizing problems whose solutions he could already see over the org's stated top priority, which he believes capped his impact ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
- Estimates his growth was 50/50 luck (landing at 2009 Facebook) versus hard work — the only factor in your control. Stop treating feedback as a grade: do something, get feedback from people whose taste you aspire to (not peers barely ahead of you), and genuinely action it ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).
