# OpenAI & Meta Distinguished Engineer (IC9) On Working With Zuck, Carmack & Career Growth | Philip Su

Philip Su is one of the rare engineers to reach Distinguished Engineer (IC9): he rose quickly to roughly IC7-equivalent at [Microsoft](../entities/microsoft.md), joined Facebook ([Meta](../entities/meta.md)) as an IC7, made IC9 by founding and scaling Facebook's London office from around a dozen engineers to 500+, and later joined [OpenAI](../entities/openai.md) before its major growth phase. The episode is packed with stories about [Mark Zuckerberg](../entities/mark-zuckerberg.md) and John Carmack, what IC9 expectations actually look like, his voluntary demotion from IC9 to IC7, and his generalist philosophy of switching between IC and EM roles. Note: this early episode's page carries only the host's written summary, not a transcript.

## Key takeaways

- What distinguished Zuckerberg, in Su's telling, was his "constant devotion to growth"; Carmack was "one of the most impressive engineers he worked with because of his prolific engineering ability" — he could drop into a codebase he knew nothing about and give excellent feedback ([working with legends](../concepts/working-with-legends.md)).
- Su reached IC9 by founding and scaling Facebook's London office from ~a dozen engineers to 500+, which gave him "the blast radius to have IC9 impact" — IC9 expectations are defined by the blast radius you have and what you can guarantee the success of, e.g. being responsible for the success of a 500+ engineer site ([career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)).
- The highest-level [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md) are not a checklist of quantitative points: they're driven by qualitative feedback, and "you need the judgment of someone at a higher level to explain what makes that person's work special."
- Su switched between IC and EM six times in his career; for generalists there's a lot of commonality between [management](../concepts/management.md) and tech leadership, which kept the door open to switch roles based on business need and personal enjoyment.
- He eventually requested a demotion from IC9 to IC7 because he wanted to do more technical work — it relieved the pressure of IC9 expectations and preserved his flexibility to switch roles ([non-linear careers](../concepts/non-linear-careers.md)).
- On generalists vs specialists: generalists are much more effective at smaller companies; once companies get larger, specialists have more potential since they can go as deep as they want.
- His advice for [writing well](../concepts/teaching-and-communication.md): "to write well, you need to read examples of good writing" and take inspiration from it to improve your own.
