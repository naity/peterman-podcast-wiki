# Instagram Staff (IC6) Promo Despite 10 Team Switches in 9 Years (Career Story)

Sash Zats grew from IC4 to Staff Engineer (IC6) at [Meta](../entities/meta.md) despite switching teams 10 times in 9 years — an unusually non-linear path through Newsfeed Delight, experimental hardware, the [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md) blockchain team, Threads pre-launch, and AI prototyping teams. The episode is a case study in optimizing for fun and zero-to-one ambiguity rather than [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md), and in how prototyping builds a reputation that pulls opportunities toward you.

## Key takeaways

- Sash repeatedly chose teams by "pure fun" rather than impact, treating [career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md) as a byproduct; he was hired at IC4 and reached IC6 "despite his best efforts not to" focus on leveling.
- Comfort with ambiguity is an asset: many strong engineers need clear specs, so being effective in poorly defined zero-to-one spaces (a habit from his startup/design background) differentiated him at [Meta](../entities/meta.md).
- On an experimental hardware team he landed only ~2 diffs in 6 months; the team was explicitly reviewed on de-risking directions rather than landing code — big tech protects risk-takers from the downside of [calibrations](../concepts/calibrations-and-ratings.md) even if it doesn't guarantee promos.
- He got recruited to Threads pre-launch because of his internal prototyping reputation: over Christmas break he stood up an internal Instagram server next to a Mastodon instance and wrote throwaway code making Instagram users resolvable over ActivityPub, then stripped images out of the Instagram iOS app to mock a text-first feed. "You can have 500 decks or you can have one prototype that works."
- Threads went from first prototype to public launch in roughly 5–6 months, built on Instagram's battle-tested primitives with a tiny team, timed against the chaos of Musk's Twitter acquisition; it became Meta's poster child for shipping big products with small teams.
- The Instagram blockchain team (NFT display/creation, exploring Mosseri's "decentralized creators" TED talk idea) embedded him with partners like Google, Spotify, Shopify and Nike, teaching him that products fail on business incentives more often than technology; crypto winter killed the project.
- The best designer partnerships are tight iteration loops with bidirectional constraint-sharing — he once built a hacky Figma-to-unreleased-hardware rendering pipeline so designers could see designs on-device instantly.
- Prototypes are the highest-fidelity communication medium: his GPT-3-era RAG demo over Facebook's help-center FAQ drew ~50 cold reach-outs from across the company, showing how demos drive [visibility and influence](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md) in ways documents can't.
- On metrics vs. taste: Goodhart's law means any elected metric gets gamed, and many valuable things aren't measurable — but large companies need frameworks "because nothing else scales."
- Advice to his younger self (via a manager): don't fix all your weaknesses — keep them out of your way, but position yourself where your strengths shine ([regrets and advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md)).
- He left Meta for [startups](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md) because it's a two-way door: financially irrational, but with no partner team to blame, you learn what your ideas are actually worth — and big tech will always hire you back.
