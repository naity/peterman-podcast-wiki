# Instagram Principal Engineer (IC8) on Promotions, Breaking Prod, Tech Leading | Jake Bolam

Jake Bolam is a Principal Engineer (IC8) at [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md), who joined [Meta](../entities/meta.md) as a Staff Engineer (IC6), initially struggled on a team that didn't value his frontend skillset, then took off after switching to an Instagram team that did — ultimately leading a rewrite of Instagram's backend that affected hundreds of teams. The episode covers his path from IC6 to IC8, his unusually permissive approach to code review, and the personal systems that let him work ~40 hours a week at Principal level. Note: this early episode's page carries only the host's written summary, not a transcript.

## Key takeaways

- On diff review, Jake biases toward "how can I accept this diff" rather than "how can I reject this diff": if a diff isn't risky or touching a critical path, he prioritizes speed and gives a rudimentary review, constantly pushing the envelope on project speed unless he gets feedback he's moving too fast — a distinctive take on [big tech culture](../concepts/big-tech-culture.md) around code review.
- His [promotion](../concepts/promotions.md) to IC8 came from a migration that started multi-team and grew in scope until it involved moving all of Instagram's backend to an entirely new backend, affecting hundreds of teams; the blast radius plus project success despite the risk earned the promotion.
- His advice to his younger self is "go where you're valued": his rough start at Meta came from joining a team that didn't value his frontend skillset, and his [career growth](../concepts/career-growth.md) took off once he moved to an Instagram team that did.
- Despite IC8-level responsibility, he works about 40 hours a week by relying on systems built over his whole career — blocking out focus time and minimizing meetings far more aggressively than the average engineer.
- His note-taking system is a pile of deliberately unorganized plain text files in VS Code, navigated by keywords he knows exist in them and cross-linked to each other like a personal Wikipedia knowledge base.
