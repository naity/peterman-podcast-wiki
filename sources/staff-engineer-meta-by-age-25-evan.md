# Staff Engineer @ Meta by Age 25 | Evan King

Evan King went from Junior (IC3) to Staff (IC6) at [Meta](../entities/meta.md) in three years, reaching Staff by age 25 — one of the fastest trajectories on the podcast. This first episode of The Peterman Post walks through each promotion in his career, how leadership opportunities and impact-focused (rather than technically complex) projects drove his advancement, why he eventually left big tech for startups, and what he'd do differently.

## Key takeaways

- Both Evan and host Ryan identify as "code machines" — engineers whose edge is high output rather than prodigy-level natural talent. Evan started programming late in high school without feeling naturally gifted ([career-growth](../concepts/career-growth.md)).
- Leadership responsibility accelerated his growth: his manager gave him borderline engineering-management responsibilities to scale a growing team, and the soft skills he'd built founding Cornell's Hacking Club proved instrumental ([mentorship-and-sponsorship](../concepts/mentorship-and-sponsorship.md), [influence-and-leadership](../concepts/influence-and-leadership.md)).
- Impact trumps technical complexity for [promotions](../concepts/promotions.md): his Staff promotion came from a simple project with extraordinary results, not technical sophistication — mirroring Ryan's own promotion experience.
- Path recap: CTF interest and founding Cornell's hacking club (solving real problems, not resume-padding projects) got him to Junior; promotion within his first half by pursuing projects outside his core scope; Mid-to-Senior via a leadership role at the edge of his capabilities that built tech-lead skills.
- His Senior-to-Staff case came after the Christchurch incident, when he led the creation of Meta's real-time integrity team — visibility plus impact justified the promotion ([incident-management](../concepts/incident-management.md)).
- "Startups promote faster technical growth than big tech does" — big tech offers depth, startups provide breadth ([startups-and-founding](../concepts/startups-and-founding.md), [big-tech-culture](../concepts/big-tech-culture.md)).
- An after-hours crypto side project gained 10k+ users and ultimately led him to leave big tech for entrepreneurship.
- Regrets: prioritizing work so heavily early in his career that it limited friendships and self-reflection, and taking shortcuts for faster promotions that sacrificed deep technical understanding he later needed ([regrets-and-advice](../concepts/regrets-and-advice.md)).
