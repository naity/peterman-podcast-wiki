# Amazon Principal Engineer On Layoffs, Interviewing & Career Growth | Steve Huynh

**March 1, 2025**

**By Ryan Peterman and Steve Huynh**

Steve Huynh followed an unconventional route into technology, beginning with a Liberal Arts degree before joining Amazon through a friend's referral. After nearly two decades at Amazon, he progressed from Support Engineer to Principal Engineer, then launched his career development YouTube channel, A Life Engineered.

## Key Takeaways From the Conversation

**Interview Preparation Reality**

Most interview advice overemphasizes coding and system design. According to Huynh, "these tests just get you in the door." Behavioral interviews determine leveling and cultural fit. Engineers should focus on articulating their experience in ways that match the target level.

**Market Dynamics for Engineers**

Junior engineering positions face limited demand, while Senior+ roles experience extraordinary demand. Competition concentrates in pre-screening phases rather than during interviews themselves.

**Amazon's Performance Culture**

The company maintains an explicit up-and-out policy for SDE1 roles requiring promotion within a timeframe, otherwise resulting in termination. This requirement relaxes at higher levels. Huynh notes that layoffs typically affect those lacking cultural fit rather than poor performers.

**Reaching Principal Engineer**

The promotion requires nearly two levels' worth of contribution simultaneously. Engineers often face conflicting feedback—told to adopt Principal behaviors while simultaneously needing stronger individual contributions. Starting preparation early proves critical.

**Avoiding Layoffs**

Direct communication with managers about expectations matters most. Early, transparent feedback prevents surprises.

## Career Insights

Huynh's promotional project involved overhauling Prime Video's outdated catalog infrastructure system, affecting hundreds of dependent engineers—a high-leverage initiative attracting senior-level investment.

He advises frequent job-hopping early in careers for rapid advancement, but recommends stability once reaching Senior+ levels for meaningful growth opportunities.

Reflecting on his journey, Huynh regrets not switching teams earlier and wishes he'd focused less on people-pleasing and external expectations.
