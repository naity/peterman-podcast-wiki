# OpenAI & Meta Distinguished Engineer (IC9) On Working With Zuck, Carmack & Career Growth | Philip Su

Host: Ryan Peterman — Published: May 23, 2025

## Host intro (as relayed by fetch; near-verbatim with quoted fragments)

Ryan Peterman introduces Philip Su as "one of the rare software engineers who reached the Distinguished Engineer (IC9) level." His early tenure at Facebook gave him "interesting stories about people like Mark Zuckerberg and John Carmack."

Career trajectory: rapid advancement to approximately IC7 (Senior Staff Engineer equivalent) at Microsoft, joining Facebook as an IC7, then promotion to Distinguished Engineer (IC9), and subsequently joining OpenAI before its major growth phase.

Su achieved IC9 "by founding and scaling the company's London office," growing "the team there from around a dozen engineers to 500+" which gave him "the blast radius to have IC9 impact."

The full podcast is on YouTube, Spotify, Apple Podcasts.

## Takeaways (headings verbatim; bodies near-verbatim with quoted fragments)

1. "Working with Mark Zuckerberg and John Carmack" — Philip Su highlighted what distinguished Zuckerberg: his "constant devotion to growth," supported by personal anecdotes. Regarding John Carmack, Su described him as "one of the most impressive engineers he worked with because of his prolific engineering ability." Su noted that "Carmack could drop into a codebase he knew nothing about and give excellent feedback."

2. "How to write well" — Philip Su, an excellent writer, shared his primary recommendation: "to write well, you need to read examples of good writing." Reading quality writing enables taking "inspiration from that writing to improve your own."

3. "What IC9 expectations look like" — Expectations at the highest levels depend on "the blast radius you have and what you can guarantee the success of." Su's IC9 example involved responsibility "for the success of a 500+ engineer site."

4. "Qualitative feedback drives the highest-level promotions" — Promotion at top levels "is not a checklist of quantitative points." Su emphasized that "you need the judgment of someone at a higher level to explain what makes that person's work special."

5. "IC vs EM commonalities for generalists" — Philip Su "switched between IC and EM six times in his career." He noted "for generalists, there is a lot of commonality between management and tech leadership," which "kept the door open and allowed him to switch roles based on business need and personal enjoyment."

6. "Getting demoted from IC9 -> IC7" — Philip Su "eventually requested a demotion to IC7 because he wanted to do more technical work." The demotion "helped relieve him from the pressures of the high expectations of IC9" and allowed role switching.

7. "Where generalists are better than specialists" — Su believes "generalists are much more effective at smaller companies." However, "once companies get larger though, specialists have more potential since they can go as deep as they want."
