# Staff Engineer @ Meta by Age 25 | Evan King

**Date:** Jan 17, 2025

**Authors:** Ryan Peterman and Evan King

---

## Overview

Evan progressed from Junior (IC3) to Staff (IC6) at Meta in three years. This conversation explores how he achieved rapid advancement through leadership, impact-focused projects, and strategic career decisions.

## Key Takeaways

**Code Output Over Natural Talent**
Both participants identified as "code machines" producing high output rather than prodigies. Evan began programming late in high school without feeling naturally gifted.

**Leadership Acceleration**
Evan's manager provided borderline engineering management responsibilities to scale the growing team. His soft skills—demonstrated through founding Cornell's Hacking Club—proved instrumental in advancement.

**Impact Trumps Technical Complexity**
The Staff promotion stemmed from a simple project with extraordinary results, not technical sophistication. This mirrors the author's own promotion experience.

**Technical Learning Environments**
Evan emphasizes that "startups promote faster technical growth than big tech does." Big tech offers depth; startups provide breadth.

---

## Career Progression Summary

**Cornell to Junior Engineer**
Evan's interest in CTF and founding the hacking club built essential soft skills. He solved real problems rather than padding resumes with random projects.

**Junior to Mid-Level**
Within his first half, he received promotion. He pursued projects outside his core scope, demonstrating agency in seeking opportunities.

**Mid-Level to Senior**
His manager placed him in a leadership role at the edge of his capabilities, developing tech lead skills essential for higher-level advancement.

**Senior to Staff**
Following the Christchurch incident, Evan led real-time integrity team creation, gaining visibility and impact that justified promotion.

**Transition to Startups**
An after-hours crypto project gained 10k+ users, eventually leading him to leave big tech for entrepreneurship and faster technical growth.

---

## Personal Reflections

Evan expressed regrets about prioritizing work early in his career, limiting friendships and self-reflection. He also acknowledged taking shortcuts for faster promotions, sacrificing deep technical understanding that later proved necessary.
