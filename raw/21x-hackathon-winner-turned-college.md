# 21x Hackathon Winner Turned College Dropout | Jia Chen Interview

**Date:** Apr 13, 2025

**Author:** Ryan Peterman and Jia Chen

---

## Introduction

Jia Chen is a 21-year-old who won 21 hackathons and co-founded her own startup while being a content creator. She succeeded in tech despite attending a non-target school and recently dropped out to work on her startup, Sprint.dev.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Why most people struggle to win hackathons** – Winning often comes down to team dynamics, judge interaction, and smart project execution, not just stacking credentials or showing off a complicated tech stack.

2. **Succeeding at a non-target school** – If your school isn't an outlier, make sure you are. Take on leadership roles, drive big projects, build a personal brand, etc.

3. **Dropping out of college** – College can provide structure but stifle creativity. Don't just drop out, though–set preconditions. Build projects, test ideas, and create opportunities first if you are considering it.

---

## Getting Into Hackathons, Strategy, Benefits

- **Building hackathon teams** – Audrey picks teammates based on skills and drive, not credentials. She looks at past projects, GitHub activity, and ambition rather than resumes. She's won with community college students who had less experience but stronger determination.

- **Judge interaction** – Keep presentations short and instead spend time having judges interact with the project. Many strong teams lose by spending excessive time discussing their tech stack.

- **Technical development** – Dividing into frontend and backend teams then merging later often fails. Connect both early and iterate together.

- **Benefits of hackathons** – Beyond resume projects, hackathons offer networking opportunities. Recruiters attend larger hackathons searching for future interns and fast-track candidates for internship applications.

---

## Standing Out From a Non-Target School

- **Agency** – Jia emphasized developing "agency"—the ability to take action and think on your feet. Hackathons helped her develop this capability and taught time efficiency with a "get it done and move on" mindset.

- **Becoming a "decision maker"** – Show leadership in clubs, spearhead big projects, or create content to stand out.

- **Is college important?** – College provides structure if you don't know what to learn, but isn't necessary. It was helpful for Jia but dulled her creativity and learning passion.

- **Personal brand** – Jia began vlogging hackathon experiences encouraged by a friend. She focuses on authenticity rather than planned content.

---

## Dropping Out, Building a Startup

- **Should you drop out?** – Before dropping out, set preconditions for your idea. Evaluate technical skills, investment networks, and other factors to verify good prospects.

- **Move fast, but avoid burnout** – Emphasize moving quickly and continuously shipping while maintaining balance.

- **Staying open** – Be receptive to connections and learning opportunities, even if they don't seem immediately beneficial.
