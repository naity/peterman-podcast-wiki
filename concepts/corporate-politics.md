# Corporate politics

The maneuvering layer of big companies: empire building, reorgs with hidden motives, calibration horse-trading, credit fights, and the wording that gets you what you want without getting you labeled a threat. Guests split between those who say the game is unavoidable and must be played well, and those who insist that goaling on the game itself is corrosive.

## What the guests say

### Is politics avoidable — or is it just how big organizations work?

[Ethan Evans](../entities/ethan-evans.md), a retired [Amazon](../entities/amazon.md) VP who devoted an entire episode to the politics he witnessed, argues politics and [influence](influence-and-leadership.md) are literally the same skill set, distinguished only by motive — "Darth Vader had executive presence in spades" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). [Joakim Recht](../entities/joakim-recht.md)'s discovery at [Uber](../entities/uber.md)'s senior levels was scarier: "nobody's in control, there's an illusion of control... just people randomly getting ideas and pushing them out"; he coped by saying no a lot and using the Denmark timezone as a shield against San Francisco hallway politics ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). [John Myles White](../entities/john-myles-white.md) concludes you can't unilaterally disarm inside a promo-driven org — "it's totally irrational not to play the game" — but you retain agency in choosing teams whose culture minimizes it ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).

The refuseniks push back. [Bryan Cantrill](../entities/bryan-cantrill.md) calls Sun's Distinguished Engineer election a "country club" vote — "the wrong place for democracy" — and escaped its politics only because the CTO personally championed his case; his fix at Oxide was abolishing ranks entirely ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)). [Mike Stonebraker](../entities/mike-stonebraker.md) built his career in academia and [startups](startups-and-founding.md) specifically to avoid big-company "bosses, rules, and politicking" ([episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)). And [Bobby Holley](../entities/bobby-holley.md) warns that overtly working the system backfires: promo-hacking is "extremely annoying" to leadership, and "you don't want the people in leadership to find you to be annoying" ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). [Stefan Mai](../entities/stefan-mai.md) offers a manager's synthesis: politics is situational, not constant — a team that feels it's winning has almost none, and zero-sum behavior festers exactly when winning slows down ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).

### The hidden machinery ICs never see

Evans catalogs the mechanics: empire building persists because headcount is the one promotion metric nobody can debate (some Amazon orgs required ~80-90 reports for director — "a several hundred thousand dollars a year bonus quite literally for accumulating 90 people"); reorgs are "hidden chess" whose public business narrative coexists with private motives like parking people, platforming favorites, or shrinking someone out; and companies run "forward looking slates" and promo quotas that create literal queues for senior slots ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). [Laurent Charignon](../entities/laurent-charignon.md) exposes the [calibration](calibrations-and-ratings.md) equivalent: untold, locally-evolved rules — like an automatic "meets expectations" if you change roles less than ~2.5 months before cycle end — one of which cost him a likely staff [promotion](promotions.md) ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). Recht, from years on promo committees, describes his first one reviewing hundreds of engineers in a single day with no materials, decided purely by how well each manager pitched: "if you have a bad manager, you have a bad case" ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Mai adds low-performer quotas (roughly 7-15% pushed into the bottom bucket at both Amazon and [Meta](../entities/meta.md)) and the manager-of-managers "mutiny" dynamic, where leaf managers advance by displacing the person above them via skip-level griping ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).

The machinery extends upward and outward. [Carey Nachenberg](../entities/carey-nachenberg.md) was made Symantec Fellow by a company-wide committee without applying, then leveled *down* by [Google](../entities/google.md) ("we can't just hire fellows") ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)). [Ryan Olson](../entities/ryan-olson.md) discovered that even an IC8 building the product can't see the real game: [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md)-era strategy calls that baffled him only made sense years later when antitrust-lawsuit memos revealed Facebook's fear of Instagram cannibalizing it ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)). Between companies, Holley recounts Google's "year of a hundred oopses" — Download-Chrome banners shown to Firefox users on google.com, always framed as unintentional, decay driven by organizational incentives rather than villainy ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). [Igor](../entities/meta-ic7-demotion-guest.md)'s Atlanta-office story shows the sponsor layer: a few hundred people cut because one senior VP pulled out — "I'm just a cell in the spreadsheet" ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)). The anonymous [consultant-turned-chief-architect](../entities/asian-dad-energy.md) treats reading "the topology of the client's political environment" as a core professional skill, and used his own network intel to swap himself onto a [layoff](layoffs.md) list in place of junior engineers ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).

### Does great work speak for itself?

This is the sharpest disagreement. Evans argues it emphatically does not: quiet, dutiful high performers get unconsciously deferred for resources because management assumes they'll accept less — "you are a very nice person... and that is not a compliment in this context" — and demanding performers with explicit deals get served first ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). [Ricky](../entities/ricky-staff-google.md) agrees from the IC side: people decide promotions, so visibility and self-advocacy are non-optional ([episode](../sources/industry-secrets-we-wish-we-knew.md)). Nachenberg learned it the hard way — he drove Lyft's shift to a neural-network driver purely through influence, then lost the credit because he let others tell the story; his rule now is to document accomplishments and claim specific, unembellished credit ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)). [Adrien Friggeri](../entities/adrien-friggeri.md) engineered his IC8 promo openly — socializing with VPs a cycle early, recruiting a critical D2 as a mentor six months ahead so there was "no debate in the room" — while insisting this isn't *just* a social game: the underlying work has to be real, consensus-building simply de-risks its recognition ([episode](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)).

Cantrill counters that goaling on the game produces performative work and miserable title-holders ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)), and Recht advises engineers to "just relax about that part and focus on providing value — the other thing will come," while conceding committees are structurally unfair to people with bad managers or isolated teams ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Holley's compromise: create visibility framed as credit for the team's work, never self-promotion — "people can tell the difference" ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). [Brian Attwell](../entities/brian-attwell.md) attacks the problem structurally: ladders that reward "influencing other teams" select for loud political operators, so CloudKitchens rebuilt its ladder to reward truth-seeking — being right when questioning foundational decisions ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).

### Pushing hard without blowing yourself up

Evans was fired twice for abrasiveness before formulating "strategically annoying": push your opinions, but if people stop disagreeing to your face they organize behind your back; his signature move is unassailable wording — "my career is very important to me, and I need to know if it's as important to Amazon" — which carries a quit-threat's payload without the extortion frame ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). [Michael Bolin](../entities/michael-bolin.md)'s E9 promotion at Meta was delayed after he pushed too aggressively (and correctly) that Nuclide's Atom foundation would lose to VS Code — the coaching was about *how* he pushed, not what he argued; he now recognizes his triggers and routes hard pushes through managers rather than acting in the moment ([episode](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md)). [Michael Novati](../entities/michael-novati.md) similarly calls his popular internal newsletter a mistake: off-the-cuff posts about second-class job families and poaching forced executives into damage control, even though Zuckerberg personally told him to keep posting ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)).

On tactics: Charignon says never fight a bureaucratic rule in the calibration room where reputations are staked — go one-on-one, gather evidence, and first learn the story behind the rule ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). Evans's mutiny rule: never complain about a bad manager alone (a lone report is cheaper to dismiss than believe); two or three corroborating reports flip the skip's calculus, and an escape attempt should start as a blame-free business case ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). Against a vindictive manager, he's blunt: "you're bringing a knife to a gunfight... HR is not there to rescue you" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). Recht warns that top-down authority without demonstrated understanding earns polite nods and silent non-compliance — his best political outcome was hearing someone champion, as their own conviction, an idea they'd once rejected from him ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). [David Fowler](../entities/david-fowler.md) adds intelligence-gathering: before changing roles, ask when the last reorg was — reorgs are executive "motion" that reveal where the org is heading — and evaluate any move by what you'd hate, not what you'd love ([episode](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)). Defending scope from raiding orgs, Evans advises bundling unglamorous work into the story, responding fast and thornily, and being the better storyteller — attackers underestimate distant teams because "respect between two engineering teams is inversely proportional to their distance" ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- State what you want in unassailable language: express career urgency as a question about mutual importance, never as a threat — delivery and timing determine how identical words land ([Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- Never mutiny alone: corroborating voices flip a skip-level's cost-benefit; a lone complaint gets you labeled the problem ([Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).
- Dispute unfair rules one-on-one with evidence, after learning the rule's origin story — never in the calibration room ([Charignon](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Build consensus before the decision meeting: socialize your case with deciders and critics cycles early so there's "no debate in the room" ([Friggeri](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)).
- Document your accomplishments and claim specific, unembellished credit — undocumented influence evaporates ([Nachenberg](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)).
- When you must push against power, manage the *how*: know your triggers, don't respond in the moment, route through managers ([Bolin](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md), [Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- If the game disgusts you, change arenas rather than pretending it doesn't exist: pick teams, ladders, or company types that reward truth-seeking over positioning ([White](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md), [Attwell](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md), [Stonebraker](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)).
