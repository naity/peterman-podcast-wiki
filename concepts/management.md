# Management

In a podcast dominated by senior-IC career stories, management shows up as a distinct craft rather than a default promotion: whether to become a manager at all, the IC/manager pendulum, the frontline-manager-to-director jump, how much managers should still code, and the machinery managers run behind closed doors — [calibrations](calibrations-and-ratings.md), PIPs, reorgs, and death marches.

## What the guests say

### Should you become a manager at all?

[David Singleton](../entities/david-singleton.md) (ex-[Stripe](../entities/stripe.md) CTO, ex-[Google](../entities/google.md) VP) first tries to talk people *out* of management: it is a different job, should be done full time rather than bolted onto IC work, and only fits if you genuinely draw energy from getting more out of other people ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)). [James Cowling](../entities/james-cowling.md) goes further — don't enter management too early (ideally reach staff first, or you'll lack the technical depth to influence strategy later), and *wanting* management too much is itself a red flag ([episode](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)). [Adam Ernst](../entities/adam-ernst.md) never considered it, arguing for honest self-assessment of archetype: he loves writing code and is weaker at alignment-driving ([episode](../sources/meta-distinguished-eng-ic9-on-influencing.md)).

Against this caution, [Laurent Charignon](../entities/laurent-charignon.md) argues most engineers passively hope for what they want: he pitched his own transition into management at [Airbnb](../entities/airbnb.md) by going to his overloaded manager with a value proposition instead of waiting to be asked ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). [Brian Attwell](../entities/brian-attwell.md) illustrates the cost of ambivalence — he procrastinated on committing to management so badly that a report asked, three months in, whether Brian was actually his boss; once he committed, his org doubled every six months ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).

### The IC/manager line is a two-way door — but hybrids pay a price

Several guests treat the tracks as a pendulum. [Ilya Grigorik](../entities/ilya-grigorik.md) frames IC vs manager as "tours of duty" chosen per problem: he became Director of Developer Relations at Google specifically to compress years of ecosystem adoption for his browser-standards work, then went back ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)). [Philip Su](../entities/philip-su.md) switched between IC and EM six times and even requested a demotion from IC9 to IC7 to do more technical work ([episode](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)). [Simon Kindström](../entities/simon-kindstrom.md) tried management as a deliberate experiment at [Meta](../entities/meta.md), found it pulled him away from technical work amid organizational thrash, and transparently switched back ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)); Charignon likewise returned to IC once his team ran itself and boredom set in ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). [Dwayne Reeves](../entities/dwayne-reeves.md) became a tech-lead-manager reluctantly and returned to IC with a resource argument: replacing a manager for 6–7 people is easier than replacing a tech lead for 35–40 ([episode](../sources/tech-lead-for-metas-most-used-programming.md)).

[Ryan Olson](../entities/ryan-olson.md) is the counterexample who fought the separation itself: as a rare Tech Lead Director at [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md) he rejected Meta's IC/manager split (preferring Tesla's flexible model) — and paid for it in ratings: "you're exceeding as an IC but you're meeting as a manager, so you're just meeting" ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).

### Managing managers is a different job — again

[Rong Yan](../entities/rong-yan.md)'s formula for the frontline-manager-to-director gap is "directing is not managing": directors add value through strategy, cross-department resource gathering, and solving the org's hardest problems, not by knowing the most details — he underperformed for six months until he made that shift ([episode](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)). [Stefan Mai](../entities/stefan-mai.md) adds the political dimension: assume managing managers is like managing engineers "and it'll just be mutiny" — in flat orgs, leaf managers advance by displacing the person above them via skip-level griping, and two teams with overlapping charters will undercut each other rather than run a clean experiment ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). [Fiona Fung](../entities/fiona-fung.md) ([Anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md), ex-Meta Senior Director) treats manager-of-managers as an explicit partnership — divide and conquer by strengths — plus a "trust but verify" muscle so the "this is fine" dog meme never becomes reality ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).

At the top, levers get binary: Mai notes you cannot do an underperforming manager's job for them — at Zuckerberg-and-SVP level, underperformance simply means being let go ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). [Ethan Evans](../entities/ethan-evans.md) (retired [Amazon](../entities/amazon.md) VP) describes the executive toolkit candidly: reorgs are "hidden chess" whose public business narrative coexists with private motives (retaining stars with juicier scope, shrinking a disfavored leader), and empire-building persists because headcount is the only promotion metric nobody can debate ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)).

### Must managers stay technical?

This is one of the sharpest disagreements. Rong Yan says everyone in engineering must stay technical — as a CTO he still writes and reviews code weekly, a lesson from Facebook bootcamp where a VP-level hire sat next to him fixing bugs for six weeks ([episode](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)). [Marc Brooker](../entities/marc-brooker.md) is blunter for the AI era: "if you aren't doing it hands on, your opinion about it is very likely to be completely wrong" — influence-role leaders must get back to hands-on building, which takes humility for people with fancy titles ([episode](../sources/aws-distinguished-eng-learnings-from.md)). [David Fowler](../entities/david-fowler.md) blocks a minimum of 8 hours a week to think and build ("decline, decline, decline") and still sends daily pull requests as a [Microsoft](../entities/microsoft.md) Distinguished Engineer ([episode](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)), and [Joakim Recht](../entities/joakim-recht.md) insists engineers at *every* level write code, ideally daily, or lose the system knowledge that makes their designs realistic ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Fung's version is dogfooding as a manager's "maker time" — using Visual Studio to build Visual Studio, selling household items to seed Marketplace, now "using Claude Code to build Claude Code" ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).

[James Everingham](../entities/james-everingham.md) pushes the opposite emphasis: technology is a creative job, so hire people smarter than you, focus them on outcomes, clear blockers, and get out of the way — micromanaging smart people loses them, and he micromanages only briefly to fix broken teams ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)). [David Ronca](../entities/david-ronca.md) learned the same restraint the hard way: two weeks into directing a well-functioning ~60-person Meta video org, a report told him "you've been here two weeks, you're already telling us what we need to do" — if a team executes well, "your job is to not break it first and then help it move forward second" ([episode](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)).

### Delegation: three rules that contradict each other

[Boris Cherny](../entities/boris-cherny.md) follows Andy Grove's rule: delegate the thing you *like* and know well, because then you can monitor it — "you never delegate the thing you don't want to do" ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)). Recht argues nearly the reverse: delegate the hard, interesting work down to people who can barely manage it so they grow fast, and keep the boring "shoveling shit" work yourself, since you have nothing left to prove and it earns enormous trust ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Kindström's rule sits orthogonally: "delegate, don't abdicate" — his failure mode was abandoning ownership of others' outcomes and scrambling at review time ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)). Cowling adds that leading by example is a delegation trap: when he jumped on every page to model ownership, his team concluded that was "James's job" — teach explicitly and delegate ownership instead ([episode](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)). Fowler's version of the transition: as senior-level co-architect of .NET Core he tried to co-review every change from ~30 engineers, burned out within a year, and was forced to learn trust and letting people fail ([episode](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)).

### The machinery: ratings, PIPs, and the company-first rule

Charignon states the untold rule: "as a manager, you represent the interest of the company. You have to put the company first before your report." His craft answer is the "surprise factor" metric — he and each report wrote the expected rating on paper before every review and revealed simultaneously; any mismatch, including positive surprises, counted as a coaching failure ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). Mai defends the expensive apparatus (~$15,000 per engineer per cycle by his estimate) because "the integrity of the performance review process is the whole ball game," while confirming low-performer quotas of roughly 7–15% are universal at scale and that PIPs are "first and foremost a legal paper trail" that people nonetheless genuinely escape ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). Evans counters from the Amazon VP chair: every PIP'd person he ever coached was fired anyway — spend the PIP period job hunting — and a manager can fire any single person they choose by striking first with the narrative (700 pull requests can be spun as upleveling the team or as nitpicking) ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). Mai also punctures a common assumption: managers are not layoff-proof — span-of-control reductions put them first on the chopping block ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md); see [layoffs](layoffs.md)).

Two guests show the manager's power used for good. [John Myles White](../entities/john-myles-white.md) used his authority over ratings to stop a team from shipping a goal-hitting change on July 1 and deleting it July 2 ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). Ronca calls Meta's data-driven calibrations the most valuable thing the company taught him — the objective, individually-credited performance management that [Netflix](../entities/netflix.md)'s flat, team-first culture lacked — and handing a report a Redefines Expectations rating "the greatest joy" of his leadership career ([episode](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)).

### Intensity vs. sustainability

Mai's first week at Amazon began with a manager committing the team to a 6–8-month nights-and-weekends death march ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)), and Attwell defends extreme hours outright — career growth vs. hours is superlinear, and 90–100-hour weeks made him Senior Staff at [Uber](../entities/uber.md) by 25, though he once collapsed in the street walking home ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)). Ronca is the direct counter: Patty McCord told him in his own Netflix interview "blow us away with what you can do in an eight hour day," and he later *ordered* a burned-out engineer to take vacation regardless of outages — the team stabilized the system precisely because they stepped back; his own 24/7 startup years ended in stage-3 colon cancer ([episode](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)). Fung splits the difference: war rooms buy focus but tax morale, and her regret from a 2+-month Marketplace lockdown is not being upfront with the team about how long it would take ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).

### A good manager is a career multiplier — verify yours

Guests repeatedly credit (or blame) individual managers for career inflections: Kindström's manager recruited him by explaining the business impact and coached him to two top ratings ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)); [Vlad Feinberg](../entities/vlad-feinberg.md)'s manager pushed him off first-author papers and into unglamorous work that snowballed into leading Gemini pre-training ([episode](../sources/google-deepmind-pre-training-lead.md)); [Evan King](../entities/evan-king.md)'s manager handed him borderline-EM responsibilities that built his Staff case ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)); [Bobby Holley](../entities/bobby-holley.md)'s manager made him spend one day a week without opening a terminal ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). But Evans warns the dependency cuts both ways — he cost a star engineer a promotion by not knowing Amazon's promo-cycle deadlines, so reports should verify their manager actually knows the process ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)), a point [Steve Huynh](../entities/steve-huynh.md) echoes: communicate directly with your manager about expectations before surprises become terminal ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- Try management full time or not at all, ideally somewhere that allows a trial-and-return — and only if other people's growth energizes you ([Singleton](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)); reaching staff-level depth first preserves your strategic credibility ([Cowling](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)).
- If you do want the job, pitch it as a value proposition to your overloaded manager instead of waiting to be tapped ([Charignon](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Run the "surprise factor" test: if you and your report would write down different expected ratings before a review, your coaching has already failed ([Charignon](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Moving to manager-of-managers, stop optimizing for team happiness — "perfectly happy teams are rarely performing at their very best potential" — and manage low performers ([Singleton](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)); expect mutiny dynamics if you treat it like managing engineers ([Mai](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
- Pick a delegation rule deliberately: delegate what you know so you can monitor it ([Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)) or delegate the growth work and keep the grunt work ([Recht](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)) — but never abdicate accountability for the outcome ([Kindström](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)).
- Inheriting a well-functioning team, shut up and build trust before proposing changes ([Ronca](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)).
- If you're put on a PIP, treat the time as a job search, not a redemption arc ([Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)) — though survivors do exist because the paper trail needs credibility ([Mai](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)).
