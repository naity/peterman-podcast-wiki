# Small teams

The recurring claim — made most forcefully by Instagram veterans — that deliberately tiny, talent-dense teams ship faster and better than large ones, even inside giant companies.

## What the guests say

Ryan Olson is the theme's strongest advocate: "if you want to go fast, go small." He *cut* the Instagram Stories team down to a core of two iOS engineers with no dedicated server engineer, and shipped in ~2-3 months a launch polished enough to beat Snapchat at everyday sharing — full ownership and zero coordination overhead, at the cost of 16-18 hour days ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)). His IC8 project, IG Labs, was a deliberately tiny high-talent-density innovation group (initially just him and one designer) that produced collaborative posts and seeded Threads ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)). He even endorses the Thanos-snap hypothetical: Instagram would likely run better with half the people, because each marginal person adds invisible coordination and codebase cost — he was waiting 4+ hours a day on builds before he left ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).

James Everingham, then Instagram's head of engineering, tells the same Stories story from the management side: the project succeeded *because* the team shrank — small, talent-dense, founder-led, with clear ownership and no cross-org dependencies — governed by Mike Krieger's value "do the simple thing first" to prove a thesis cheaply before scaling ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)). Sash Zats supplies the sequel: Threads went from first prototype to public launch in ~5-6 months with a small team building on Instagram's primitives, becoming Meta's poster child for lean product building ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)) — a team drawn from the IG Labs talent pool Olson describes ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- When speed matters, shrink the team until ownership is total and coordination is near zero (Olson [episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).
- Structure innovation teams for talent density and independence from the org chart, not headcount (Olson [episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md); Everingham [episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- Prove the thesis with the simple thing first; scale the team only after (Everingham [episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)).
- Build on existing primitives to keep the team small — Threads' 5-6-month launch rode Instagram's infrastructure (Zats [episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)).
