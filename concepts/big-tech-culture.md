# Big Tech culture

How life inside Meta, Google, Amazon, Microsoft and their peers actually works: the incentive systems, the intensity, the surprising degree of permissionless freedom, and the ways scale corrodes both. It is the podcast's most contested terrain — guests disagree sharply on whether these companies reward impact or optics, and on whether the machine deserves your loyalty.

## What the guests say

### Does the system reward impact — or optics?

The bleakest account comes from [John Myles White](../entities/john-myles-white.md), who ran [promotions](promotions.md) in [Meta](../entities/meta.md)'s AI Infra: literally everyone's first goal was promo, teams shipped projects "everyone agreed... was bad" because it was their promo bar, and systems were deliberately maximally coupled to hit scope requirements ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). [Brian Attwell](../entities/brian-attwell.md) diagnoses the root cause: scope-indexed ladders reward "position-oriented, politics-rewarding competencies like influencing other teams" — being loud and empire-building — which is why CloudKitchens rebuilt its ladder around truth-seeking after Travis Kalanick rejected an [Uber](../entities/uber.md)-style draft as lazy ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).

Guests split on how to respond. [Bryan Cantrill](../entities/bryan-cantrill.md) refuses to play at all — "I would much rather be under promoted than over promoted... I am not interested in doing work performatively" — and calls stack ranking "organizational cancer" that teaches you your team are adversaries ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)). [Bobby Holley](../entities/bobby-holley.md) agrees from the leadership chair: promo-hacking is "extremely annoying" to leadership, and impact-framed ambition is what actually carried him to Distinguished Engineer at [Mozilla](../entities/mozilla.md) ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). But [Ricky](../entities/ricky-staff-google.md) (Staff at [Google](../entities/google.md) by 28) counters that quality work alone is not enough — people decide promotions, so visibility and self-advocacy are part of the job ([episode](../sources/industry-secrets-we-wish-we-knew.md)) — and [Ethan Evans](../entities/ethan-evans.md) confirms from the [Amazon](../entities/amazon.md) VP chair that leaders really do promote the polite agitator over the patient team player, because retention triage is real ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). White stakes out the middle: inside a promo-driven org "it's totally irrational not to play the game," but you can choose teams — PyTorch selected for love of craft and held a bar so high its IC8s out-classed other orgs' IC10s ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).

Several guests reframe the whole apparatus as machinery, not truth. [Ryan Olson](../entities/ryan-olson.md) calls levels "an incentive system... the carrot that's being dangled in front of you," with promo delays often pacing rather than performance ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)); [Rong Yan](../entities/rong-yan.md) says "levels and titles are things the company gives you; they are not yours" ([episode](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)); [Sash Zats](../entities/sash-zats.md) notes big tech will even protect risk-takers from rating downside — his experimental team landed ~2 diffs in 6 months and was reviewed on de-risking — but Goodhart's law degrades any metric the system chooses ([episode](../sources/instagram-staff-ic6-promo-despite.md)). The machinery can also simply fail to pay out: [Jayendra Jog](../entities/jayendra-jog.md) got a 5/5 rating at Robinhood after months of 2 AM nights with no promotion or comp change attached, and deliberately started coasting ([episode](../sources/quitting-robinhood-and-raising-35m.md)).

### Hours and intensity: the only way up, or a trap?

Attwell is the maximalist: 90-100 hour weeks prototyping his team's daily debates made him de facto tech lead within months, and "if you want to be senior staff at one of the world's best tech companies by the time you're 25, this is the only way to do it" — though he also once collapsed in the street walking home from Uber ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)). [Stefan Mai](../entities/stefan-mai.md)'s first week at Amazon opened with a manager committing the team to a 6-8 month nights-and-weekends death march; he calls 50-60 hour weeks fairly common for the ambitious ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)), and Olson worked 16-18 hour days shipping [Instagram](../entities/instagram.md) Stories with a deliberately tiny team ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).

[David Ronca](../entities/david-ronca.md) is the sharpest counter: [Netflix](../entities/netflix.md)'s Patty McCord told him in his own interview "we don't value 24/7 work here... blow us away with what you can do in an eight hour day," and his stage-3 colon cancer diagnosis after years of 10am-to-3am startup crunch convinced him balance must be baked in early ([episode](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)). [Jake Bolam](../entities/jake-bolam.md) proves seniority doesn't require the grind — he works ~40 hours a week at IC8 via ruthless focus-time systems ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-engineer-ic8.md)). And [Igor](../entities/meta-ic7-demotion-guest.md) argues Meta's version of intensity is partly theater: deliberately aggressive arbitrary deadlines slip, "everybody is fine with that," and people learn the pressure is artificial — "like elementary school, where the teacher is yelling but kids are still playing" ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)).

### The cultures are genuinely different

Cross-company contrasts recur. Igor, who worked at both, says Google (at least historically) applied pressure only in exceptional cases where Meta manufactures it constantly; he also found Meta's senior IC scope "crowded" and its onboarding of external senior hires broken, because nearly all its senior people grew up internally ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)). Mai, who managed at both Amazon and Meta, says their performance systems converge (public ratings plus a private linchpin process) but the money diverges — Meta pays its top quintile dramatically more, some clearing 2x peers, while frugal Amazon claws it back; Amazon HR was "savage" box-checking where Meta HR exhausted every avenue before firing ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). Attwell is blunter still: Amazon-style companies underpay top performers "because they can get away with it," and Google managers told him of an unwritten policy of retaining idle smart people so competitors couldn't hire them — his "entropy law" says standards fall until a company dies or a trauma resets them ([episode](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)). [Steve Huynh](../entities/steve-huynh.md) adds Amazon's explicit up-or-out policy for junior engineers, and his observation that Amazon [layoffs](layoffs.md) hit cultural misfits more than poor performers ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).

Culture also changes over time and with leadership. [David Fowler](../entities/david-fowler.md) lived [Microsoft](../entities/microsoft.md)'s transformation from Ballmer-era competing-teams shouting matches to Satya-era incentivized collaboration, where reviews dinged you for cloning another team's work ([episode](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)). [Fiona Fung](../entities/fiona-fung.md) contrasts four-week Visual Studio sprints with one-week Facebook Marketplace sprints and the credo "nothing at Facebook is somebody else's problem," and says [Anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md)'s safety mission was evident from day-one onboarding, not just a tagline ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)). [Michael Novati](../entities/michael-novati.md) recalls early Facebook as extremely engineer-driven — "move fast and break things" meant breaking norms, and PMs had to win engineers over ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)). Ronca says Netflix's famous flat, no-levels, team-first culture was real but didn't scale: without leveling nobody could rationalize pay gaps, and without individual credit the best engineers eventually leave ([episode](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)). [James Everingham](../entities/james-everingham.md) credits Instagram's talent density with "IQ-ing past" the usual ~100-person communication breakdown ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)), [David Singleton](../entities/david-singleton.md) modeled [Stripe](../entities/stripe.md)'s nested ops reviews directly on Amazon's — culture as something you deliberately design ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)) — and [Michael Bolin](../entities/michael-bolin.md) distinguishes research-led [OpenAI](../entities/openai.md) ("if the model weren't very good, it wouldn't really matter what we did on the harness") from engineering-led Meta ([episode](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md)).

### "You can just do things": permissionless agency

A striking consensus: big companies grant far more bottom-up freedom than their bureaucratic image suggests. Fowler's whole career ran on "code is currency" — build the prototype first, show it around — and "the more success you have, the more leeway you get to fail" ([episode](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)). [Brendan Burns](../entities/brendan-burns.md) says you can hide roughly 10% of your effort from management and should always run a self-directed bet ([episode](../sources/the-creator-of-kubernetes-on-building.md)). [Boris Cherny](../entities/boris-cherny.md) volunteered his product team as infra's guinea pig without asking permission and credits arriving *under*-leveled with the freedom to build ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)); Singleton's junior-engineer act of agency saved ~70 years of user time per year ([episode](../sources/stripe-cto-on-what-grew-his-career.md)). [Adrien Friggeri](../entities/adrien-friggeri.md) offers a caveat that cuts against Burns's stealth advice: for multi-month bets like Bento he negotiated explicitly-agreed perf risk with his manager and skip, arguing over-communication, not concealment, is what makes exploration survivable in today's harsher climate ([episode](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)). [Evan King](../entities/evan-king.md) adds the limit case: big tech gives depth, but startups still promote faster technical breadth ([episode](../sources/staff-engineer-meta-by-age-25-evan.md)), and [Carey Nachenberg](../entities/carey-nachenberg.md) notes the prestige ceiling — Google leveled him down from a Symantec Fellow-equivalent (L10) to L8 because "we can't just hire fellows" ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)).

### Scale costs, and the end of loyalty

Olson answers the Thanos-snap hypothetical with a yes — Instagram would likely run better with half the people, since each marginal engineer adds hidden coordination and codebase cost (he waited 4+ hours a day on builds before leaving) ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)). [Joakim Recht](../entities/joakim-recht.md) saw Uber's early "let builders build" freedom become "an insane strategy that's going to kill you" at scale — a global tracing mandate was re-emailed for ~3 years and never got done ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)).

On loyalty, the veterans converge on disillusionment. Igor's formative moment was Google's Atlanta office shutdown — a few hundred people cut because one senior VP sponsor pulled out: "at the end of the day, I'm just a cell in the spreadsheet" ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)). Novati left when Meta's all-in mutual loyalty faded into a business relationship ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)); White is "very bullish on Meta as a stockholder" but bearish on being an employee, now that perceived labor oversupply has ended retention sacrifices ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)); the anonymous [chief architect](../entities/asian-dad-energy.md) laid off after 25 years argues current cuts are partly stock-pumping "AI efficiency" PR ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)). Evans describes zombie products kept alive because shutdown admits failure, with VPs rated well for hitting interim goals on doomed visions ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)); Cantrill watched Sun colleagues lobby to get *onto* the layoff list for severance and quit Oracle in 45 days out of shame ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)). [Mike Stonebraker](../entities/mike-stonebraker.md) opted out of the whole arrangement, choosing academia plus [startups](startups-and-founding.md) because big companies impose "bosses, rules, and politicking" ([episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)) — while [Dwayne Reeves](../entities/dwayne-reeves.md) offers the counter-testimony of staying ~14 years at Meta because he consistently felt valued and outside offers never fixed the actual, solvable problem ([episode](../sources/tech-lead-for-metas-most-used-programming.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- Pick high-growth, meritocratic companies; the company's trajectory is your escalator ([Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md), [Attwell](../sources/cloudkitchens-cto-on-intelligence.md)).
- When in doubt, take the lower level on the way in: under-leveled means room to exceed and compounding reputation; over-leveled means racing a calibration clock you may not beat ([Ronca](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md), [Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md), [Igor](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)).
- Use permissionless agency — prototype first, ask later — but for long exploratory bets, negotiate explicit perf cover with your manager and skip rather than hiding them ([Fowler](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md), [Burns](../sources/the-creator-of-kubernetes-on-building.md), [Friggeri](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)).
- Treat the level ladder as the company's incentive machinery, not your identity: titles belong to the company, and promo delays are often pacing ([Rong Yan](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md), [Olson](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md), [Cantrill](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)).
- Learn to distinguish real urgency from manufactured urgency, and protect a sustainable pace — crunch as the exception, systems for focus time ([Igor](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md), [Ronca](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md), [Bolam](../sources/instagram-principal-engineer-ic8.md)).
- Assume the relationship is a business one from day one: build savings, keep your network warm, and don't confuse employer branding with loyalty ([Novati](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md), [Igor](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md), [ADE](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
