# Mentorship and sponsorship

Two related but distinct relationships: mentors teach and advise; sponsors stake their own credibility on your advancement. The episodes cover how to get both, how to be both, and where guests disagree about formality and technique.

## What the guests say

### Sponsorship: the relationship that actually moves careers

The clearest sponsorship stories are transactional and explicit. Ethan Evans's "magic loop" VP promotion was a 2.5–3 year explicit partnership — "I'll do everything you need done; you make sure I'm rewarded for it" — culminating in his manager staking personal credibility on the promo case; as a leader he ran the same deal from the other side, getting a senior manager to director by having him build Amazon's India dev center over three years ([episodes](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md), [corporate politics follow-up](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). Adrien Friggeri engineered sponsorship for his IC8 case, asking a critical D2 for monthly mentoring six months ahead so there was "no debate in the room" ([episode](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)). Austen McDonald notes referrals work the same way in hiring: a VP who worked with you stakes reputation and can advocate inside the committee room ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)). Simon Kindström's rocket ride to IC6 was manager-powered throughout — a manager with deep org knowledge who recruited him by explaining how the work's business impact would convert into career growth ([episode](../sources/26-year-old-meta-staff-eng-ic6-on.md)). Carey Nachenberg generalizes: "a manager can make or break your career and your life and your happiness" — find one who trusts you and gives you rope ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)).

### How to get a mentor: ask directly, or never ask?

A genuine disagreement of technique. James Everingham: "don't go and ask someone, hey, I need a mentor. Go offer them help" — mentorship evolves from usefulness ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)). David Fowler did nearly the opposite, twice, and it worked: he deliberately attached himself to architect David Ebbo — coding his ideas and mimicking his behaviors until he became one himself — and later formally *requested* a Technical Fellow mentor (Jeffrey Snover) once he decided he wanted Distinguished Engineer ([episode](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)). Stefan Mai's version favors explicitness too: his director knew he'd succeed the moment Mai walked in with a written list of things he didn't yet know how to do ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-manager-m2-on-manager.md)). The reconciliation may be seniority: Fowler was offering prodigious output alongside the ask — which is Everingham's "offer help" in another form.

### How to mentor: structure vs. informality

Fiona Fung distinguishes mentoring (listening plus advice) from coaching (acting as a mirror so the person discovers answers), and argues the best relationships have mentee-set goals and an explicit "what does success look like in 3/6 months" conversation ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)). Joakim Recht runs the opposite regime: keep mentoring informal — no programs, plans, or schedules — and pick mentees *outside* your own org, both to learn about distant parts of the company and to "plant seeds" of culture change; don't disqualify mentees for asking the wrong questions, "that's why they need mentoring" ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Laurent Charignon supplies the technique both camps can use: situational leadership — match style (directing, selling, supporting, delegating) to the learner's mastery and motivation; his "failing" bootcamp mentee became the strongest of four once he stopped directing and switched to supporting ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). Marius Schulz's model is the driving instructor with a second steering wheel, backing off as trust builds ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)); Ilya Grigorik's success test is disappearing "Homer Simpson into the bushes" — if the team keeps executing without him, the upleveling worked ([episode](../sources/distinguished-engineer-at-shopify.md)).

### The compounding returns

Mentorship's payoffs recur decades later in these stories. The anonymous laid-off architect names strong mentors as the driver of his best growth stretch, and his single most profound lesson ("if you can stay flexible, you'll survive") came from one ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)). Igor's intern later became the manager of the team he'd interned on — "don't underestimate your interns" ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)). Mike Stonebraker's advice to PhD grads includes finding a mentor as a career fundamental ([episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)). John Myles White's biggest regret is a sponsorship opportunity refused: never attending his skip-level's (Javier Olivan's) office hours despite weekly prompting — leaders "are starved for ground truth," and showing up is half the relationship ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)). Rong Yan's counterpoint responsibility for managers hired above resentful teams: show reports you are "a promoter of their career" and unblock what they can't ([episode](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- Distinguish mentors from sponsors, and make sure you have the latter before a big promo: someone above you who will spend credibility in the room ([Ethan Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md), [Adrien Friggeri](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)).
- To attract a mentor, lead with usefulness — code their ideas, take work off their plate — whether or not you also ask explicitly ([James Everingham](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md), [David Fowler](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)).
- Pick a person whose career you want and study them at close range; repeat at each level ([David Fowler](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)).
- As a mentor, diagnose before directing: match your style to the mentee's mastery and motivation ([Laurent Charignon](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)).
- Set explicit goals with mentees ([Fiona Fung](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)) — or deliberately keep it informal and cross-org for reach ([Joakim Recht](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)); both work, but choose consciously.
- Accept standing invitations from leaders above you; office hours are sponsorship on offer ([John Myles White](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
