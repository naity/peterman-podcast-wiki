# Imposter syndrome

Self-doubt among engineers at every level — from new hires at big tech to the creator of C++ arriving at Bell Labs and a Turing Award winner starting as a Berkeley professor — and the guests' sharply different verdicts on whether it is a cost, a signal, or a spur.

## What the guests say

### Imposter syndrome as career damage

Carey Nachenberg gives the corpus's most consequential account: imposter syndrome kept him at Symantec for 21+ years, long after he had stopped growing and being happy — "I stayed because it was comfortable and I complained. I complained all the time." Only an external pull ("we want to interview you") broke the logjam, and his biggest regret is not leaving earlier ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)). Dwayne Reeves describes a related cost: early-career imposter syndrome kept him identifying as an output machine until he gained the clarity that "I'm more than what I can type on the keyboard" — the value is in being in the rooms deciding what to build ([episode](../sources/tech-lead-for-metas-most-used-programming.md)).

### Imposter syndrome as a healthy signal

Boris Cherny inverts the framing entirely: directing engineers at his own level on Claude Code, he concluded levels don't matter at all — and "if you never feel imposter syndrome you aren't pushing yourself hard enough" ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)). David Patterson's story supports the adaptation view: arriving as a UCLA grad student turned UC Berkeley professor was "very intimidating," but deciding "I'm probably not going to get tenure, so I should just have a good time" defused it within a year ([episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-tpu-vs-gpu-vs.md)). Bjarne Stroustrup says he felt it at Bell Labs seeing the names on the doors — "maybe I still have a bit of it" — but converted it into fuel: "I thought I have to up my game, I have to do something bigger and better than what I had imagined" ([episode](../sources/creator-of-c-bell-labs-negative-overhead.md)). Simon Peyton Jones's adjacent reassurance: "all the successful people are making it up as they go along, so it's fine if you are too" ([episode](../sources/co-creator-of-haskell-functional.md)).

The tension is real: Nachenberg's experience says untreated imposter syndrome quietly steals decades, while Cherny says its absence means you're coasting. The synthesis several guests gesture at is that the feeling is fine — acting on it by shrinking (staying put, staying silent) is the failure mode.

### Debunking the comparison

Igor supplies the sharpest analytical tool: comparing yourself to a group of peers is a category error — one is great at presenting, another at leading, another at coding — so "you're measuring yourself against a composite no individual matches." He adds, "I'm sure Elon Musk has imposter syndrome" ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)). Joakim Recht discovered the deflationary truth on arrival at Uber from small-company Denmark: the big-tech people "were not that much better than me after all... it's not rocket science, it is just computers" ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- Don't let comfort masquerade as contentment: if you're complaining constantly but staying, imposter syndrome may be the reason — and the cost compounds by the year ([Carey Nachenberg](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)).
- When you compare yourself to peers, notice you're comparing against a composite; pick one real person and one real skill instead ([Igor](../sources/meta-senior-staff-ic7-engs-honest.md)).
- Treat the feeling as evidence you're in a stretch role, not evidence you don't belong ([Boris Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- Use intimidating environments as a bar-raiser: let the names on the doors raise your ambitions rather than lower your confidence ([Bjarne Stroustrup](../sources/creator-of-c-bell-labs-negative-overhead.md)).
- Remember that even the people whose competence intimidates you are improvising ([Simon Peyton Jones](../sources/co-creator-of-haskell-functional.md), [Joakim Recht](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)).
