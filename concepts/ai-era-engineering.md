# AI-era engineering

What LLMs and agents mean for the software engineering profession — which skills survive, whose jobs change, and how careers should adapt. This is the podcast's most contested theme: tool builders and infrastructure veterans land on sharply different answers.

## What the guests say

### How big is the shift?

The optimists treat it as an epochal but continuous transition. Marc Brooker argues software has been supply-constrained for its entire ~60-year history and the mainstream will move to agentic, specification-driven development, with hand-coding persisting as craft "like hand joinery" ([episode](../sources/aws-distinguished-eng-learnings-from.md)). Michael Novati calls LLMs "the Vim-to-VSCode leap repeated" — he produces ~5x more code in a 500k-line codebase by prompting for code he already sees in his head, and speculates that long-term, AI managing its own services behind API contracts could make human-readable code irrelevant ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)). Bobby Holley says AI is coming to consumer technology "like water running downhill" — unstoppable — while warning the web could become a backend detail for agents ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). Brendan Burns thinks the attention shift is already here: "people focus so much on the AI that they forget about the Kubernetes part" ([episode](../sources/the-creator-of-kubernetes-on-building.md)).

The skeptics attack specific claims with data. Bjarne Stroustrup accepts AI for boilerplate and documentation but argues LLM code in safety- and performance-critical domains brings more bugs, bloat, and an unsolvable validation burden (prompt changes perturb whole codebases; regulators require validated changes), and skewers the perpetual "it'll be solved in the next release" promise ([episode](../sources/creator-of-c-bell-labs-negative-overhead.md)). Mike Stonebraker reports LLM text-to-SQL scores ~85% on academic benchmarks but 0% on his real-world warehouse benchmark Beaver (~10% with RAG), because real enterprise data isn't in the training pile and schemas are messy ([episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)). James Cowling says AI/agentic development is bad at the single hardest engineering property — simplicity — "which remains a human domain for now" ([episode](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)). David Malan's live example: a Claude session that was 90% right but hallucinated an API, "why the human should stay in charge for some time" ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).

Simon Peyton Jones stakes out a middle position: LLMs "may be the best thing that's happened to statically typed languages for a long time" because the compiler prunes the generation space in a tight loop — but "copilots need pilots," and he won't merge unreviewed AI code into GHC ([episode](../sources/co-creator-of-haskell-functional.md)).

### Will there still be jobs?

Ricky (Staff at Google) treats LLMs as productivity tools like many before them and sees SWE jobs as secure for roughly five years, with longer-term impacts unpredictable ([episode](../sources/industry-secrets-we-wish-we-knew.md)). Vlad Feinberg's argument against "permanent underclass" doom: accountability keeps humans essential — "you can't hand off blame to AI... they can't represent you in court because they can't be disbarred" — and planning around stochastic AI components is itself the differentiating skill ([episode](../sources/google-deepmind-pre-training-lead.md)). The anonymous chief architect laid off after 25 years offers the cynical counterpoint: companies under pressure to show returns on AI investment cut headcount to keep revenue flat and earn "AI efficiency" PR that pumps the stock — AI as layoff cover as much as cause ([episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)). Stroustrup raises the pipeline problem nobody answers: if AI eliminates junior programmers, "where do you get the senior programmers from?" ([episode](../sources/creator-of-c-bell-labs-negative-overhead.md)). Charlie Marsh echoes it from the other side: being an early-career engineer now is very hard, because the strongest engineers are also the best agent users ([episode](../sources/openai-eng-and-dev-tools-founder.md)).

### What skills survive?

Remarkable convergence here, even across the optimist/skeptic divide:

- **Fundamentals bound the agent's output.** Michael Bolin — whose code is now 80-90% model-written — insists deep technical skills matter because "your questions bound the agent's output quality," and recommends reading an OS textbook cover to cover ([episode](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md)). Malan argues you won't *use* first principles but must *know* them — they distinguish an engineer from a coder whose output AI can already generate; the point of CS was never programming but problem solving ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)). Charlie Marsh: "Being a great software engineer is more useful than ever" — the strongest engineers on his team are the most effective agent users ([episode](../sources/openai-eng-and-dev-tools-founder.md)).
- **Judgment and taste.** Novati says near-term AI amplifies experienced engineers with strong taste ([episode](../sources/meta-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on-zuck.md)); Boris Cherny holds model-written code to exactly the same bar as human code and still hand-writes his core query loop ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)).
- **Thinking, not delegating it.** Holley's rule: do not let an LLM write for you — "the process of writing is the process of thinking" and outsourcing it forfeits the growth ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). Cowling is glad his PhD's training in sitting with uncertainty predated LLMs as a crutch ([episode](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)).
- **Staying hands-on, even at senior levels.** Brooker: "if you aren't doing it hands on, your opinion about it is very likely to be completely wrong" — influence-role leaders must return to building with agentic tools, which takes humility ([episode](../sources/aws-distinguished-eng-learnings-from.md)). Fiona Fung lived this: using Claude via Meta's internal DevMate convinced her AI is "already here" and pulled her to Anthropic, where she ships production software again ([episode](../sources/anthropic-eng-leader-and-ex-senior.md)).
- **If coding gets fully solved, product skills.** Carey Nachenberg: at "AGI-good" coding, the valuable skills become outcome analysis, customer understanding, and communicating requirements — being a genuinely competent product manager ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)).

### Career strategy in the AI economy

James Everingham warns many AI startups are "building for the future past," filling gaps LLMs will soon close (his example: Cursor as autocomplete) — "you build a product on a bus stop, not where the end of the line is"; his own bet, Guild, is deterministic governance for enterprise agents, needed no matter which model company wins ([episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md)). Nimit Sohani maps the AI-startup landscape: research-only startups are all-or-nothing against resource-rich labs, product-only wrappers have no moat, and the durable position is the product-research intersection ([episode](../sources/stanford-phd-ai-researcher-and-quant.md)). Bryan Cantrill's contrarian caution: boom/bust cycles are endemic to software, so be your own judge and don't pick a space just because it's hot ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)). For getting into the field, Feinberg says the best signal is public evidence you built something useful — e.g. contributions to [open-source](open-source.md) serving stacks like vLLM or SGLang ([episode](../sources/google-deepmind-pre-training-lead.md)). Hiring is adapting too: Austen McDonald reports agentic coding has made end-to-end ownership the key behavioral signal for mid-level candidates, and AI labs screen for values — OpenAI's "Feel the AGI" optimism vs Anthropic's "Hold Light and Shade" risk articulation ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)). David Patterson supplies the hardware backdrop: the end of Dennard scaling and slowing of Moore's Law forced the ~2015 turn to domain-specific architectures exactly as ML arrived as the obvious target ([episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-tpu-vs-gpu-vs.md)).

Education is renegotiating its AI contract: Malan bans off-the-shelf ChatGPT/Copilot on CS50 problem sets but gives unlimited access to a deliberately less-helpful tutor ([episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)); Nachenberg is banning LLMs on projects after project scores diverged sharply from exam understanding ([episode](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)); Peyton Jones still wants every child to learn that ChatGPT is "a trillion floating-point numbers plus arithmetic" ([episode](../sources/co-creator-of-haskell-functional.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- Use agents heavily but hold their output to the same bar as human code; keep hand-writing what is safety- or correctness-critical (Cherny [episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md); Bolin [episode](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md)).
- Invest in fundamentals — OS internals, first principles, math — because your understanding bounds what you can get out of an agent (Bolin [episode](../sources/openai-codex-tech-lead-on-how-his.md); Malan [episode](../sources/harvard-professor-cs50-what-matters.md)).
- Never delegate your thinking: write your own documents, review what you merge (Holley [episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md); Peyton Jones [episode](../sources/co-creator-of-haskell-functional.md)).
- Senior leaders should get hands-on with agentic tools before opining on them (Brooker [episode](../sources/aws-distinguished-eng-learnings-from.md)).
- Benchmark AI claims against your real data, not academic leaderboards (Stonebraker [episode](../sources/turing-award-winner-postgres-disagreeing.md)).
- Build for where the model will be, not for today's gaps that the next model closes (Everingham [episode](../sources/ex-head-of-eng-at-instagram-career.md); see also Cherny's "build for the model six months from now" in [ai-coding-tools](ai-coding-tools.md)).
- Treat "AI efficiency" layoff narratives with skepticism — some are financial engineering (Asian Dad Energy [episode](../sources/laid-off-from-big-tech-after-25-years.md)).
