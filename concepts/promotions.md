# Promotions

How engineers actually get promoted at big tech companies — the hidden mechanics of promo committees, sponsorship, scope, and business need — and the podcast's liveliest running argument: whether you should optimize for promotion at all.

## What the guests say

### The mechanics: promotions are lagging, sponsored, and rationed

Marius Schulz gives the cleanest model: ratings measure delivered impact, while promotions (which lag) measure *sustained next-level behaviors* — he was blocked once for a single behaviors miss despite greatly exceeding expectations, and blocked again by a company-wide freeze ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)). At IC7+ delivery alone is not enough: there must be "an IC7-shaped hole that can only be plugged by an IC7-shaped puzzle piece" — a business need ([same episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)). Ethan Evans reveals the supply side: large companies run "forward looking slates" of promotions over 6–24 month windows, and quota systems (e.g. Google allowing two L8→L9 promos per half per org) create literal queues ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-reveals-everything-hes.md)). Sponsorship is load-bearing: Evans's own VP promotion was a 2.5–3 year "magic loop" partnership in which his manager staked personal credibility on the case ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)), and Joakim Recht — a promo-committee veteran — says his first committee reviewed hundreds of engineers in one day, decided purely by how well each manager pitched: "if you have a bad manager, you have a bad case" ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)).

At the top of the ladder the process changes character entirely. Sun's Distinguished Engineer promotion was a "country club" vote by existing DEs ([Bryan Cantrill](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)); Symantec ran a CTO-led company-wide committee reviewing portfolios quarterly ([Carey Nachenberg](../sources/googlex-chief-scientist-on-imposter.md)); Microsoft's DE promotion is peer review by Technical Fellows of a decade-long body of work ([David Fowler](../sources/intern-to-microsoft-distinguished.md)); and Philip Su says the highest promotions run on qualitative judgment — "you need someone at a higher level to explain what makes the person's work special" ([episode](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)).

### Scope is the currency

Joakim Recht's model: level tracks how many people are affected by your work, so a project with natural scope expansion "drags promotions along automatically — for you and the whole team"; each of his Senior→Principal→Distinguished steps followed a stage of Odin's growth ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Marius Schulz's "there's an IC4, IC5, and IC6 way to solve almost any problem" makes the same point at task granularity ([episode](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)); Philip Su reached IC9 because founding Facebook London gave him "the blast radius to have IC9 impact" ([episode](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)); Jake Bolam's IC8 came from a migration whose blast radius grew to all of Instagram's backend ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-engineer-ic8.md)); Steve Huynh notes Amazon's Principal promotion demands nearly two levels' worth of contribution simultaneously ([episode](../sources/amazon-principal-engineer-on-layoffs.md)).

### The core disagreement: play the promo game, or refuse it?

This is the sharpest fault line among guests.

**The refusers.** Bryan Cantrill: "I would much rather be under promoted than over promoted" — he never goaled on rank, promotions "just happened," and he prescribes finding meaning instead (he can introduce you to miserable Distinguished Engineers) ([episode](../sources/distinguished-eng-on-stack-ranking.md)). Bobby Holley warns that promo-hacking is "extremely annoying" to leadership — "you don't want the people in leadership to find you to be annoying" ([episode](../sources/mozilla-firefox-cto-on-browser-war.md)). Rong Yan: don't make promotions the objective — titles belong to the company, control impact and treat the rest as lagging indicators ([episode](../sources/frontline-manager-at-meta-to-senior.md)). Joakim Recht: "just relax about that part and focus on providing value — the other thing will come" ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). James Cowling is angriest: promotion systems that reward complexity partly drove him to found Convex ([episode](../sources/dropboxs-former-most-senior-eng-building.md)).

**The witnesses to promo culture's damage.** John Myles White ran promotions in Meta's AI Infra, where "literally everyone's first goal was promo": teams shipped projects everyone agreed were bad and built maximally coupled systems to hit scope bars — promo culture decoupled promotion from skills that compound. But he refuses the purist conclusion: "it's totally irrational not to play the game" inside a promo-driven org; your real agency is *choosing teams* (PyTorch's high bar made its promo cases more credible) ([episode](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).

**The deliberate engineers.** Adrien Friggeri engineered his IC8 promo openly: co-writing the packet, socializing with VPs a cycle early, and recruiting a critical D2 as a mentor six months ahead so there was "no debate in the room" ([episode](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)). Ryan Olson, as a mis-leveled IC4, deliberately asked for an impossible 4-to-6 double promo knowing HR would refuse — seeding the idea so it converted into back-to-back promotions; his frame is that levels are "an incentive system, a dangled carrot" and delays are often pacing, not performance ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)). Ethan Evans pushed with deliberately unassailable wording ("my career is very important to me, and I need to know if it's as important to Amazon") and admits leaders promote the polite agitator over the patient team player because retention triage is real ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).

Boris Cherny stakes out a middle path: coming in *under-leveled* was lucky (space to build without box-checking, compounding reputation from exceeding expectations), his IC8 promo was "a non-event driven by his manager," and levels exist for company reasons, not engineering reasons ([episode](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md)). David Ronca agrees from the hiring side: when in doubt take the lower level — over-leveled and struggling "there's no way to fix that problem" ([episode](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md)). Austen McDonald counters that you can and should negotiate level during hiring — get the loop run at the target level and anchor every interviewer there ([episode](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)) — a genuine tension with the take-the-lower-level camp.

### Unfairness is structural, not incidental

Beyond bad managers, Joakim Recht catalogs unavoidable unfairness: dependencies that ghost you, projects canceled from above, isolated teams never told what good looks like ([episode](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md)). Laurent Charignon lost a likely staff promotion to an untold local calibration rule (role change < ~2.5 months before cycle end = automatic "meets expectations") ([episode](../sources/airbnb-staff-eng-on-how-to-not-get.md)). Evans once cost a star SDE1 a promotion simply by not knowing Amazon's twice-yearly cycle — the report bears the risk, so verify your manager knows the process ([episode](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)). Ryan Olson's dual TL/manager role averaged his ratings down ("exceeding as an IC but meeting as a manager, so you're just meeting") ([episode](../sources/instagram-principal-eng-ic8-on-building.md)).

## Practical takeaways

- Do next-level work *before* the promo and keep doing it through setbacks; promotions reward sustained demonstration, not a good half ([Marius Schulz](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md)).
- Secure a sponsor, not just a supporter: someone who will stake credibility in the room. Make the deal explicit if you can ([Ethan Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md), [Adrien Friggeri](../sources/new-grad-to-principal-engineer-ic8.md)).
- Verify your manager knows the promo process, its cycles, and its deadlines — the report pays for the manager's ignorance ([Ethan Evans](../sources/amazon-vp-on-promotions-getting-fired.md)).
- Attach yourself to work with natural scope expansion; the level follows the blast radius ([Joakim Recht](../sources/uber-distinguished-eng-on-unfair.md), [Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)).
- If your org runs on promo culture, don't unilaterally disarm — but choose teams whose bar you respect ([John Myles White](../sources/msl-eng-director-promo-hacking-industry.md)).
- Joining a company, prefer the level you can visibly exceed; if you do negotiate level, do it during the loop, not after ([David Ronca](../sources/retired-netflix-engineering-director.md), [Boris Cherny](../sources/boris-cherny-creator-of-claude-code.md), [Austen McDonald](../sources/meta-hiring-lead-on-behind-the-scenes.md)).
- Expect IC7+ to require a business need and an advocate above your level; without both, delivery stalls ([Marius Schulz](../sources/instagram-senior-staff-eng-ic7-on.md), [Philip Su](../sources/openai-and-meta-distinguished-engineer.md)).
